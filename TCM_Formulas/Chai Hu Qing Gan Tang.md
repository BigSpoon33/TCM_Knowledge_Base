### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Liver fire rising with shaoyang disorder (especially in children)
**Key Symptoms**: Fever with chills, ear pain and swelling, lymph node swelling, sore throat, irritability, red eyes, headache, bitter taste, constipation
**Tongue**: Red with yellow coating
**Pulse**: Wiry and rapid

### Secondary Applications
- Acute otitis media in children
- Lymphadenitis and neck swelling
- Acute conjunctivitis
- Childhood fever with irritability
- Mumps and parotitis
- Acute pharyngitis and tonsillitis

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Pediatric ear infections and otitis media
- Lymphatic inflammation and swelling
- Acute eye infections and conjunctivitis
- Childhood febrile illnesses
- Viral infections with liver fire signs
- Autoimmune conditions with inflammation

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
Classic pediatric formula for liver fire with shaoyang disorder. Particularly effective for children who develop ear infections, swollen glands, and irritability with fever. The combination addresses both the pathogen (shaoyang) and constitutional tendency (liver fire). Essential pattern includes alternating symptoms with local inflammation.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and immune-modulating effects. Chai Hu provides hepatoprotective compounds while heat-clearing herbs reduce inflammation. Studies demonstrate efficacy in pediatric infections, lymphatic inflammation, and fever reduction.

### Clinical Experience
First-line formula for pediatric ear infections and febrile illnesses with liver fire signs. Particularly effective for children who are irritable, have red faces, and develop local swelling. Often combined with external applications for ear problems. Excellent safety profile for children.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 3-6g granules twice daily (pediatric doses)
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, can be given as frequent small doses
**Timing**: Every 2-4 hours during acute phase, then twice daily
**Duration**: 3-7 days for acute infections, reduce dose as symptoms improve
