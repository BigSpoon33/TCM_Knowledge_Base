**Pulse**: Deep and choppy, or rapid and forceful

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Tao He Cheng Qi" means "peach pit to order the qi" - using Tao Ren (peach kernel) to move blood while Da Huang orders qi downward. Classic formula for acute lower abdominal blood stasis with heat accumulation. Represents emergency treatment for serious abdominal conditions with blood stasis component.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and bowel-regulating effects. Tao Ren contains compounds that improve microcirculation and reduce inflammation. Da Huang provides anthraquinones with purgative and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies demonstrate benefits in acute abdominal conditions, appendicitis, and intestinal disorders.

### Clinical Experience
Powerful formula for acute abdominal emergencies with blood stasis signs. Often used in hospital settings for appendicitis, intestinal obstruction, and post-surgical complications. Requires careful monitoring and appropriate medical supervision. Best results when used early in acute conditions with proper medical oversight.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses 1-2 times daily for acute conditions
**Preparation**: Da Huang added near end of cooking, Mang Xiao dissolved in strained decoction
**Timing**: Take on empty stomach for maximum purgative effect
**Duration**: Short-term use only for acute conditions, 1-5 days typically
