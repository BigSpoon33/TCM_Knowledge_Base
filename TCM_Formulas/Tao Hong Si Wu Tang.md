### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Blood deficiency with blood stasis
**Key Symptoms**: Irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhea with clots, amenorrhea, postpartum abdominal pain, chronic pelvic pain, purple complexion, dark menstrual blood with clots
**Tongue**: Purple or with purple spots, possible thin coating
**Pulse**: Choppy or wiry and choppy

### Secondary Applications
- Postpartum blood stasis with lochia retention
- Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease
- Endometriosis with blood stasis signs
- Chronic headaches from blood stasis
- Trauma recovery with blood stasis
- Chronic skin conditions with poor circulation

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Dysmenorrhea and menstrual irregularities

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
Perfect formula for blood deficiency complicated by blood stasis. The Si Wu Tang base nourishes blood while Tao Ren and Hong Hua gently move blood and resolve stasis. Represents the balanced approach to treating blood patterns - nourish and move simultaneously.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, circulation-improving, and hormone-regulating effects. Tao Ren contains amygdalin with anti-inflammatory properties. Hong Hua provides safflower yellow with circulation-promoting effects. Studies show efficacy in dysmenorrhea and endometriosis.

### Clinical Experience
Essential formula for women's reproductive health issues involving both deficiency and stagnation. Often more effective than Si Wu Tang alone for menstrual disorders. Takes 2-3 cycles to see full effects on menstruation.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, can be taken as pills
**Timing**: Throughout menstrual cycle, increase during menstruation
**Duration**: 3-6 cycles for menstrual disorders
