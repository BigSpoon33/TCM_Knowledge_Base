### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Stomach fire with yin and blood deficiency
**Key Symptoms**: Toothache, swollen bleeding gums, bad breath, mouth ulcers, red swollen tongue, thirst with desire for cold drinks, facial acne along jawline, irritability, constipation
**Tongue**: Red with yellow coating, possible ulcers
**Pulse**: Rapid and strong, or rapid and thready

### Secondary Applications

- Recurrent mouth ulcers and oral inflammation

- Facial acne related to digestive heat
- Gastritis with stomach fire

### Modern Clinical Uses

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Clear the Stomach" refers to clearing fire from the stomach channel which manifests in oral and dental problems. Classic formula that balances fire-clearing with yin-blood nourishment to prevent over-drying. Essential for stomach fire patterns that affect the mouth, teeth, and gums.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and tissue-healing effects. Huang Lian contains berberine with anti-bacterial properties effective against oral pathogens. Sheng Di Huang and Dang Gui provide compounds that promote tissue healing and reduce inflammation. Studies demonstrate benefits in gingivitis, oral ulcers, and dental infections.

### Clinical Experience
Excellent for chronic oral and dental problems related to stomach heat. Particularly effective for people with recurring gum problems, mouth ulcers, or bad breath. Often provides relief within 3-5 days for acute conditions. Best combined with proper oral hygiene and dietary modifications to reduce stomach heat.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, can be used as mouth rinse (cooled)
**Timing**: Between meals, avoid hot foods and drinks during treatment
**Duration**: 1-2 weeks for acute conditions, 4-8 weeks for chronic problems
