## üéØ Clinical Applications

### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Roundworm infestation with cold-heat complex and digestive disturbance
**Key Symptoms**: Intermittent abdominal pain, vomiting (especially after eating), diarrhea alternating with constipation, cold limbs, irritability, restlessness, possible worm expulsion
**Tongue**: May be red with white coating or variable
**Pulse**: Wiry or deep and slow

### Secondary Applications
- Chronic dysentery with mixed cold-heat signs
- Irritable bowel syndrome with complex symptoms
- Chronic gastritis with mixed patterns
- Biliary dyskinesia and gallbladder disorders
- Chronic diarrhea with constitutional weakness
- Post-infectious bowel disorders

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Functional gastrointestinal disorders
- Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) with mixed symptoms
- Chronic gastritis and peptic ulcer disease
- Biliary tract disorders
- Chronic inflammatory bowel conditions
- Post-infectious gastroenteritis

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Wu Mei Wan" represents the classic approach to "cold above, heat below" patterns complicated by parasites. Wu Mei (black plum) calms roundworms while the complex herb combination addresses the underlying cold-heat complex. The formula treats both the parasites and the constitutional imbalance that allows them to flourish.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive-regulating effects. Wu Mei contains organic acids with antiparasitic properties. The warming herbs improve digestive function while clearing herbs prevent excessive heat accumulation. Studies demonstrate benefits in functional GI disorders and chronic digestive conditions.

### Clinical Experience
Excellent for complex digestive disorders with mixed cold-heat symptoms. Particularly effective for IBS and chronic gastritis with alternating symptoms. Often requires 6-12 weeks for full benefit. Best results when combined with dietary modifications and stress reduction. Modern use focuses more on functional disorders than actual parasites.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Usually taken as pills, 6-9g twice daily
**Preparation**: Traditionally made into honey pills, modern granules available
**Timing**: Between meals, can adjust timing based on symptoms
**Duration**: 6-12 weeks for chronic digestive disorders, shorter for acute parasitic infections
