### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Blood stasis in lower abdomen with yang deficiency and cold
**Key Symptoms**: Lower abdominal pain (fixed, worse with pressure), menstrual irregularities, dysmenorrhea, amenorrhea, postpartum abdominal pain, cold limbs, dark purple menstrual blood with clots
**Tongue**: Purple or with purple spots, may have white coating
**Pulse**: Deep, slow, and choppy

### Secondary Applications
- Endometriosis with cold signs
- Ovarian cysts and uterine fibroids
- Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease
- Postpartum blood stasis
- Infertility due to blood stasis
- Chronic urogenital pain

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Endometriosis and adenomyosis

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Shao Fu" refers to the lower abdomen, particularly the area below the navel where reproductive organs are located. "Zhu Yu" means "drive out stasis." Classic formula for blood stasis in the pelvic region with underlying cold and yang deficiency. The combination of blood-moving and warming herbs addresses both the stagnation and the underlying cold.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and circulation-improving effects. Dang Gui and Chuan Xiong improve microcirculation while Yan Hu Suo provides analgesic compounds. Studies demonstrate benefits in endometriosis, dysmenorrhea, and chronic pelvic pain. The warming herbs help improve local circulation and reduce inflammation.

### Clinical Experience
Excellent for women with cold constitution who develop pelvic blood stasis conditions. Particularly effective for severe dysmenorrhea, endometriosis, and chronic pelvic pain with cold signs. Often provides pain relief within one menstrual cycle. Best results when combined with lifestyle modifications to support yang qi.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, Pu Huang should be wrapped in cloth
**Timing**: Between meals, can adjust timing around menstrual cycle
**Duration**: 3-6 months for chronic conditions, often cyclical use
