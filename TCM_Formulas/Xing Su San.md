### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Exterior wind-cold with internal phlegm-dampness
**Key Symptoms**: Cough with white phlegm, chest tightness, mild chills and fever, headache, body aches, nasal congestion, poor appetite, fatigue
**Tongue**: White thick coating
**Pulse**: Floating and slippery

### Secondary Applications
- Common cold with productive cough
- Acute bronchitis with phlegm
- Asthma with exterior symptoms
- Chronic cough with phlegm-dampness
- Allergic rhinitis with cold symptoms
- Post-infectious cough

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Upper respiratory tract infections
- Acute bronchitis and pneumonia (early stage)
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) exacerbations
- Allergic asthma with cold triggers
- Post-viral cough syndrome
- Chronic rhinosinusitis

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Xing Su San" combines exterior releasing with phlegm transformation for the common pattern of catching cold while having underlying phlegm-dampness constitution. The apricot kernel and perilla combination specifically targets cough while addressing both the acute exterior condition and chronic internal dampness.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows antitussive, expectorant, and immune-modulating effects. Xing Ren contains amygdalin with cough-suppressing properties. Zi Su Ye provides volatile oils with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Studies demonstrate benefits in respiratory infections and chronic cough conditions.

### Clinical Experience
Excellent for people who get respiratory infections easily due to underlying phlegm-dampness constitution. Particularly effective for chronic cough that worsens with weather changes. Often used during transition seasons. Best results when combined with dietary modifications to reduce dampness.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, don't overcook to preserve volatile oils
**Timing**: After meals to protect stomach from Ban Xia
**Duration**: 5-10 days for acute conditions, longer for chronic cough
