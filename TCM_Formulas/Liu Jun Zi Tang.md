## üéØ Clinical Applications

### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Spleen qi deficiency with phlegm-dampness
**Key Symptoms**: Fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools, nausea, abdominal distension, chronic cough with white phlegm, dizziness, heavy sensation in body
**Tongue**: Pale with thick white greasy coating
**Pulse**: Weak and slippery

### Secondary Applications
- Chronic digestive disorders with phlegm
- Chronic bronchitis with white phlegm
- Chronic fatigue with dampness accumulation
- Morning sickness with qi deficiency
- Chronic gastritis with phlegm formation
- Poor appetite in children

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Chronic gastritis and functional dyspepsia
- Chronic bronchitis and COPD (stable phase)
- Chronic fatigue syndrome with digestive symptoms
- Post-chemotherapy digestive recovery
- Chronic diarrhea with phlegm signs
- Pediatric failure to thrive

## üìù Therapeutic Actions
**Primary**: Tonify qi, strengthen spleen, transform phlegm, regulate qi

**Secondary**: Improve appetite, stop nausea, reduce fatigue, clear mental fogginess

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Six Gentlemen" builds on the "Four Gentlemen" (Si Jun Zi Tang) by adding phlegm-transforming herbs. Perfect formula for modern lifestyle - addresses both qi deficiency and phlegm-dampness accumulation from poor diet and sedentary habits.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows gastroprotective, immunomodulating, and anti-fatigue effects. The combination of qi-tonifying and phlegm-transforming herbs shows synergistic benefits for digestive function and energy levels. Studies demonstrate efficacy in functional dyspepsia.

### Clinical Experience
Extremely practical formula for patients with chronic fatigue and digestive issues. Often more effective than Si Jun Zi Tang alone due to phlegm component. Particularly good for people who feel heavy, sluggish, and have poor digestion.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, simmer Ban Xia longer to reduce toxicity
**Timing**: Between meals for digestive symptoms
**Duration**: 4-12 weeks for chronic conditions
