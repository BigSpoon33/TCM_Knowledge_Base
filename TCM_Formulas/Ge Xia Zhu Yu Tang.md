## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Ge Xia" means "below the diaphragm" - referring to blood stasis in the lower abdomen affecting organs like uterus, ovaries, and lower digestive tract. Part of Wang Qing Ren's systematic approach to treating blood stasis in different body regions. Strong formula requiring careful diagnosis.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and circulation-improving effects. Wu Ling Zhi contains resin acids that strongly activate blood circulation. Multiple blood-moving herbs work synergistically to break up stagnation. Studies demonstrate efficacy in gynecological conditions and chronic pain.

### Clinical Experience
Powerful formula for stubborn gynecological conditions with blood stasis. Particularly effective for women with chronic pelvic pain, irregular periods, and palpable masses. Often used long-term for conditions like endometriosis and fibroids. Requires constitutional assessment before use.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Wrap Wu Ling Zhi in cloth when decocting to prevent residue
**Timing**: Between meals, can start after menstruation ends
**Duration**: 3-6 months for chronic conditions, monitor progress regularly
