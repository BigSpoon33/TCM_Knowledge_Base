## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"Tian Ma Gou Teng" represents the classic combination for liver yang rising transforming into internal wind. Tian Ma extinguishes wind while Gou Teng pacifies liver yang. The formula addresses both the root (liver-kidney yin deficiency) and the manifestation (yang rising and wind stirring). Essential for neurological symptoms from liver imbalance.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows antihypertensive, neuroprotective, and sedative effects. Tian Ma contains gastrodin with anti-convulsant and neuroprotective properties. Gou Teng provides rhynchophylline with hypotensive and sedative effects. Studies demonstrate benefits in hypertension, headaches, and movement disorders.

### Clinical Experience
Excellent for hypertension and neurological symptoms in elderly patients. Particularly effective for people with stress-related hypertension and tension headaches. Often provides blood pressure reduction within 2-4 weeks. Best results when combined with lifestyle modifications and stress reduction.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 9-12g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Gou Teng added near end of cooking to preserve alkaloids
**Timing**: Morning and evening doses, monitor blood pressure
**Duration**: 6-12 weeks for chronic conditions, often long-term maintenance
