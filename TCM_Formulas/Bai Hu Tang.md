## üéØ Clinical Applications

### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Qi level heat (Yang Ming heat) with injury to fluids
**Key Symptoms**: High fever (worse in afternoon), profuse sweating, intense thirst for cold drinks, irritability, restlessness, headache, toothache
**Tongue**: Red with yellow coating
**Pulse**: Surging and rapid, or flooding and large

### Secondary Applications
- High fever from any cause
- Heat stroke and summer heat syndrome
- Pneumonia with high fever
- Acute gastritis with heat signs
- Hypertension with heat pattern
- Diabetes with heat and thirst

### Modern Clinical Uses
- Acute febrile illnesses with high fever
- Heat stroke and hyperthermia
- Acute pneumonia and bronchitis
- Acute gastroenteritis with fever
- Hypertensive crisis with heat signs
- Diabetic ketoacidosis (as adjunct)

## üìù Therapeutic Actions
**Primary**: Clear qi level heat, generate fluids, stop thirst

**Secondary**: Reduce fever, calm restlessness, protect stomach qi

## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
"White Tiger" represents the metal element - strong, cutting, cooling force that can subdue intense heat. This is the strongest heat-clearing formula for qi level patterns. The "four big" dosage pattern (large Shi Gao dose) is essential for effectiveness.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and fluid-regulating effects. Shi Gao (gypsum) provides calcium sulfate with cooling properties. Zhi Mu contains saponins and alkaloids with fever-reducing effects. The combination demonstrates synergistic antipyretic actions.

### Clinical Experience
Extremely effective for high fevers - often reduces temperature within hours. Must use adequate Shi Gao dosage (30-60g minimum). Most effective when taken frequently during acute fever. Monitor for overcooling in weak patients.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses, take every 2-3 hours during high fever
**Preparation**: Decoct Shi Gao first for 20 minutes, then add other herbs
**Timing**: Frequent small doses better than large infrequent doses
**Duration**: Stop when fever breaks and sweating decreases
