## üí° Clinical Notes & Commentary

### Traditional Understanding
Classic formula for liver qi stagnation - the fundamental pattern in modern stress-related conditions. "Shu Gan" means to "course the liver" or relieve liver constraint. Essential for people who hold tension, worry excessively, or have irregular emotions. The formula moves stuck qi without depleting energy.

### Modern Pharmacology
Research shows adaptogenic, antidepressant, and smooth muscle relaxing effects. Chai Hu contains compounds that modulate stress hormones while qi-moving herbs improve circulation. Studies demonstrate efficacy in anxiety, depression, and stress-related gastrointestinal disorders.

### Clinical Experience
First-line formula for stress-related conditions and modern lifestyle diseases. Particularly effective for working professionals with high stress levels. Excellent for women with PMS and stress-related menstrual irregularities. Often used as base formula with specific additions for individual symptoms.

### Dosage & Administration
**Standard Dose**: Decoct above doses twice daily, or 6-9g granules twice daily
**Preparation**: Standard decoction, can increase Xiang Fu for severe stagnation
**Timing**: Between meals, can take during stressful periods
**Duration**: 2-8 weeks for acute stress, longer for chronic patterns
