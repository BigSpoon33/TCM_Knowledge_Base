## üîç How to Use These Materials

### Step 1: Assess Your Needs
- Identify which exam(s) you're taking (CALE, NCCAOM FOM/BIO/ACP/CH)
- Determine your timeline (months until exam)
- Identify your weak areas

### Step 2: Create a Study Plan
1. **Weeks 1-4**: Read primary references (Tier 1 books)
2. **Weeks 5-8**: Study knowledge reference files (herbs, formulas, points)
3. **Weeks 9-10**: Practice sample questions, identify gaps
4. **Weeks 11-12**: Review weak areas, final practice

### Step 3: Active Study Techniques
- **Flashcards**: Create for herbs, formulas, points
- **Practice questions**: Use sample bank, take NCCAOM practice exams
- **Study groups**: Discuss difficult concepts with peers
- **Self-testing**: Quiz yourself regularly
- **Clinical application**: Relate to patient cases

### Step 4: Track Progress
- Mark off herbs/formulas as you memorize them
- Keep a log of practice question scores
- Review incorrect answers thoroughly
- Adjust study plan based on weak areas

## üìö Study Tips by Content Type

### For Herbs:
- Group by category (tonifying, clearing heat, etc.)
- Learn properties (taste, temperature, channels entered)
- Focus on functions and indications
- Know processing methods (Pao Zhi)
- Practice herb pairs (Dui Yao)

### For Formulas:
- Memorize chief herbs first
- Understand formula hierarchy (Jun, Chen, Zuo, Shi)
- Know modifications for different patterns
- Practice matching formulas to clinical presentations
- Study contraindications and cautions

### For Points:
- Use anatomical landmarks
- Practice measuring cun on yourself
- Know functions and indications
- Understand point categories (Yuan, Luo, Xi-Cleft, etc.)
- Learn point combinations

### For Pattern Differentiation:
- Master tongue and pulse diagnosis
- Understand Eight Principles
- Know Zang Fu patterns thoroughly
- Practice Six Stages, Four Levels, San Jiao
- Study Five Elements correspondences

### Nomenclature Variations
- **CALE uses**: NEW CAM (Chinese Acupuncture and Moxibustion 2005, 3rd ed.)
- **NCCAOM uses**: Primary references (Bensky, Maciocia, Deadman, WHO)
- **Be aware**: Different systems may use different names for same herb/point/formula
- **Solution**: Cross-reference the nomenclature tables in each file

### Exam Updates
- Content outlines may change - check official websites

- Federal regulations take precedence over textbook information
- Always verify current requirements before exam
