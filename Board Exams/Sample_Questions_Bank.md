## Foundations of Oriental Medicine (FOM)

### FOM-1: Five Element Theory
According to Five Element theory, which taste, color, and organ are associated with Metal?

(A) bitter, red, Lung  
(B) pungent, white, Lung  
(C) spicy, yellow, Spleen  
(D) sweet, yellow, Spleen

**Answer**: B

---

### FOM-2: Pattern Differentiation - Liver
A 29-year-old patient complains of hypochondriac pain and fullness for several months. She is also experiencing dry mouth and throat, depression, moodiness, scanty menstrual flow, and breast pain. She has a pale tongue and a thready, wiry pulse.

What is the most appropriate diagnosis?

(A) Liver Fire insulting Lung  
(B) Liver Qi stagnation transforming to Fire  
(C) Liver Qi stagnation with Blood deficiency  
(D) Liver Fire attacking Stomach

**Answer**: C

---

### FOM-3: Treatment Principles - Gynecology
A patient complains of shortened menstruation with scanty, dull red, clear, thin menses. She has coldness in the lower abdomen. Her tongue is pale, tender, with white fur. Her pulse is deep and tight.

Which of the following is the most appropriate treatment principle?

(A) activate the channel and clear Heat  
(B) tonify Yang and move Blood  
(C) tonify Yin and clear Heat  
(D) warm the channel and expel Cold

**Answer**: D

## Biomedicine (BIO)

### BIO-1: Endocrinology - Thyroid
A 40-year-old patient with an enlarged thyroid gland is most likely deficient in which of the following?

(A) iodine  
(B) iron  
(C) magnesium  
(D) zinc

**Answer**: A

---

### BIO-2: Pediatrics - Dehydration
A parent reports that their active eight-year-old child has been fussy, thirsty, and tired for the past 24 hours. The parent also states that the child complains of a headache and constipation. The child's blood pressure is low with a rapid pulse.

Which of the following would most likely be suspected?

(A) anxiety attack  
(B) dehydration  
(C) food poisoning  
(D) hyperthyroidism

**Answer**: B

---

### BIO-3: Emergency Referral - Head Trauma
A lethargic, 53-year-old patient fell and hit their head six hours before their appointment. The patient now presents with confusion, difficulty remembering the event, and has vomited twice since the fall.

What is the best course of action for this patient at this time?

(A) treat the patient and recommend that they consult their physician  
(B) treat the patient and retain them in the office for observation  
(C) do not treat the patient, but refer them to a neurologist within 72 hours  
(D) do not treat the patient, but refer them immediately to the emergency department

**Answer**: D

## Acupuncture with Point Location (ACPL)

### ACPL-1: Patient Positioning
Which of the following points could be needled with the patient positioned in the prone position?

(A) Yintang (Extra)  
(B) P 2 (Tianquan)  
(C) Sp 11 (Jimen)  
(D) GB 36 (Waiqiu)

**Answer**: D

---

### ACPL-2: Needling Techniques
For which of the following conditions is the bleeding technique most likely indicated?

(A) high fever  
(B) chronic asthma  
(C) anemia  
(D) diabetes

**Answer**: A

---

### ACPL-3: Point Location - Lung Channel
Which of the following statements best describes the location of Lu 7 (Lieque)?

(A) on the forearm, superior to the styloid process of the radius, 1 cun proximal to the transverse crease of the wrist  
(B) on the forearm, superior to the styloid process of the radius, 1.5 cun proximal to the transverse crease of the wrist  
(C) on the radial side of the flexor carpi ulnaris tendon, 1 cun proximal to the transverse crease of the wrist  
(D) on the radial side of the flexor carpi ulnaris tendon, 1.5 cun proximal to the transverse crease of the wrist

**Answer**: B

## Chinese Herbology (CH)

### CH-1: Formula Indications - Tonifying
Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan (Prepared Aconite Pill to Regulate the Middle) is most indicated for which of the following?

(A) diarrhea with burning sensation and a slippery, rapid pulse  
(B) constipation with abdominal pain and a flooding pulse  
(C) constipation with hard stools and a thin, rapid pulse  
(D) diarrhea with cold extremities and a faint pulse

**Answer**: D

---

### CH-2: Formula Selection - Yin Deficiency
A 50-year-old patient presents with a chief complaint of hot flashes. She feels warmer in the evening and while sleeping. Her tongue is bright red and peeled. Her pulse is rapid and thin.

Which of the following formulas is most appropriate for this patient?

(A) Gui Pi Tang (Restore the Spleen Decoction)  
(B) Liu Jun Zi Tang (Six-Gentlemen Decoction)  
(C) Liu Wei Di Huang Wan (Six-Ingredient Pill with Rehmannia)  
(D) Si Wu Tang (Four-Substance Decoction)

**Answer**: C

---

### CH-3: Formula Modification - Wind-Heat
A patient has been taking Yin Qiao San (Honeysuckle and Forsythia Powder) for a sore throat, swollen tonsils, fever, cough, and headache. The tongue is red and the pulse is rapid and floating. Now, the patient presents with sinus congestion, frontal headache and a thick, greenish nasal discharge.

Which of the following is the most appropriate formula for the patient at this time?

(A) Cang Er Zi San (Xanthium Powder)  
(B) Ding Chuan Tang (Arrest Wheezing Decoction)  
(C) Sang Ju Yin (Mulberry Leaf and Chrysanthemum Decoction)  
(D) Wen Dan Tang (Warm the Gallbladder Decoction)

**Answer**: A

## Study Tips

### For FOM Questions:
1. Master the Five Element correspondences (organs, colors, tastes, emotions, seasons)
2. Practice pattern differentiation with tongue and pulse diagnosis
3. Understand treatment principles for each pattern
4. Know the relationships between Zang Fu organs

### For BIO Questions:
1. Study common medical conditions and their signs/symptoms
2. Understand when to refer to emergency care vs. routine medical care
3. Know basic pathophysiology and diagnostic indicators
4. Familiarize yourself with red flags requiring immediate referral

### For ACPL Questions:
1. Memorize point locations using anatomical landmarks
2. Practice measuring cun distances on yourself and others
3. Know which points can be needled in different patient positions
4. Understand needling techniques and their indications

### For CH Questions:
1. Know the chief herbs and key ingredients of major formulas
2. Understand formula modifications based on pattern changes
3. Study the indications, contraindications, and cautions for formulas
4. Practice matching formulas to clinical presentations with tongue/pulse

## Exam Day Tips

1. **Read each question carefully** - don't rush
2. **Eliminate obviously wrong answers** first
3. **Look for key words** in the question stem (e.g., "most appropriate," "best," "most likely")
4. **Consider tongue and pulse** when given in clinical scenarios
5. **Trust your first instinct** unless you find a clear error
6. **Manage your time** - don't spend too long on any one question
7. **Stay calm** - if you've studied, you're prepared
8. **Review flagged questions** if time permits
