## Primary References

These are the **most important** references that form the basis for exam questions:

### Essential Textbooks

1. **Cheng, Xinnong, ed.** *Chinese Acupuncture and Moxibustion*. 3rd ed. Beijing: Foreign Languages Press, 2009.
   - **Used for**: Point locations, pulse nomenclature, needling techniques
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (CALE and NCCAOM primary reference)

2. **Deadman, Peter, Mazin Al-Khafaji, and Kevin Baker.** *A Manual of Acupuncture*. 2nd ed. East Sussex, England: Journal of Chinese Medicine Publications, 2007.
   - **Used for**: Point locations, functions, indications
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (NCCAOM primary reference)

3. **Maciocia, Giovanni.** *The Foundations of Chinese Medicine: A Comprehensive Text for Acupuncturists and Herbalists*. 3rd ed. Philadelphia: Elsevier Churchill Livingstone, 2015.
   - **Used for**: Pattern differentiation, theory, diagnosis
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (NCCAOM primary reference)

4. **Maciocia, Giovanni.** *The Practice of Chinese Medicine: The Treatment of Diseases with Acupuncture and Chinese Herbs*. 2nd ed. Philadelphia: Elsevier Churchill Livingstone, 2007.
   - **Used for**: Treatment principles, clinical applications
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (NCCAOM primary reference)

5. **Bensky, Dan, Steven Clavey, and Erich Stöger.** *Chinese Herbal Medicine: Materia Medica*. 3rd ed. Seattle: Eastland Press, 2004.
   - **Used for**: Individual herb properties, functions, indications
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (NCCAOM primary reference for herbs)

6. **Scheid, Volker, Dan Bensky, Andrew Ellis, and Randall Barolet.** *Chinese Herbal Medicine: Formulas and Strategies*. 2nd ed. Seattle: Eastland Press, 2009.
   - **Used for**: Formula compositions, modifications, indications
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (NCCAOM primary reference for formulas)

7. **World Health Organization.** *WHO Standard Acupuncture Point Locations in the Western Pacific Region*. Manila: WHO Regional Office for the Western Pacific, 2008.
   - **Used for**: Standardized point locations
   - **Priority**: HIGHEST (International standard)

## Study Priority Guide

### Tier 1: MUST READ (Highest Priority)

These books form the foundation of exam questions:

1. **Cheng, Xinnong** - *Chinese Acupuncture and Moxibustion* (3rd ed.)
2. **Deadman** - *A Manual of Acupuncture* (2nd ed.)
3. **Maciocia** - *Foundations of Chinese Medicine* (3rd ed.)
4. **Maciocia** - *Practice of Chinese Medicine* (2nd ed.)
5. **Bensky** - *Materia Medica* (3rd ed.)
6. **Scheid/Bensky** - *Formulas and Strategies* (2nd ed.)
7. **WHO** - *Standard Acupuncture Point Locations*

### Tier 2: HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Essential for comprehensive understanding:

1. **Maciocia** - *Diagnosis in Chinese Medicine* (2nd ed.)
2. **Maclean** - *Clinical Handbook of Internal Medicine* (2nd ed.)
3. **Bickley** - *Bates' Guide to Physical Examination* (12th ed.)
4. **Chen** - *Chinese Medical Herbology and Pharmacology*
5. **CCAOM** - *Clean Needle Technique Manual* (7th ed.)

### Tier 3: SUPPLEMENTARY

Helpful for specific topics:

1. **Anzaldua** - *Medical Red Flags & Referrals*
2. **Deng** - *Practical Diagnosis in TCM*
3. **Wiseman/Ellis** - *Fundamentals of Chinese Medicine*
4. **Kastner** - *Chinese Nutritional Therapy*
5. **Legge** - *Close to the Bone*

### Study Strategy by Exam

#### For CALE:
- Focus on **Cheng** for pulse and point nomenclature
- Study herb and formula lists from appendices
- Review point locations using **Deadman** and **WHO**

#### For NCCAOM FOM:
- Master **Maciocia Foundations** for pattern differentiation
- Study **Deng** for diagnosis methods
- Review **Maciocia Practice** for treatment principles

#### For NCCAOM BIO:
- Study **Bickley** for physical examination
- Review **Merck Manual** for medical conditions
- Know **red flags** from **Anzaldua**

#### For NCCAOM ACP:
- Memorize locations from **Deadman** and **Cheng**
- Study **WHO** for standardized nomenclature
- Review **Clean Needle Technique Manual**

#### For NCCAOM CH:
- Master **Bensky Materia Medica** for individual herbs
- Study **Scheid/Bensky Formulas** for compositions
- Review **Chen** for pharmacology and interactions

## How to Use This Bibliography

### For Exam Preparation:

1. **Start with Tier 1 books** - These are non-negotiable
2. **Create a study schedule** - Allocate time for each book
3. **Focus on exam-relevant sections** - Don't try to read everything
4. **Use multiple resources** - Cross-reference difficult concepts
5. **Take notes** - Create your own study guides
6. **Practice with questions** - Apply knowledge to clinical scenarios

### For Long-Term Learning:

1. **Build your library gradually** - These are lifetime references
2. **Keep books accessible** - You'll reference them in practice
3. **Update editions** - Stay current with new editions
4. **Join study groups** - Discuss concepts with peers
5. **Teach others** - Best way to solidify knowledge

### Cost-Saving Tips:

1. **Check your school library** - Many books available for free
2. **Buy used editions** - Older editions often sufficient
3. **Share with classmates** - Split costs and study together
4. **Digital versions** - Often cheaper than print
5. **Prioritize** - Buy Tier 1 first, borrow Tier 2-3

## Important Notes

1. **Federal regulations take precedence** over information found in other references
2. **Nomenclature may vary** between texts - NCCAOM uses standardized terms from primary references
3. **Exam content evolves** - Check NCCAOM website for updates
4. **Practice exams** are available from NCCAOM - highly recommended
5. **Clean Needle Technique** certification required - study the manual thoroughly
