## Primary Source Books (Must Have)

### For Herbs
**Bensky, Dan, Steven Clavey, and Erich St√∂ger.** *Chinese Herbal Medicine: Materia Medica.* 3rd ed. Seattle: Eastland Press, 2004.
- **Use for**: All individual herb properties, functions, indications, contraindications
- **Organization**: By therapeutic category
- **Page range**: ~1100 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

**Alternative:**
**Chen, John K., and Tina T. Chen.** *Chinese Medical Herbology and Pharmacology.* City of Industry, CA: Art of Medicine Press, 2004.
- **Use for**: Additional herb information, pharmacology, interactions
- **Organization**: By therapeutic category
- **Page range**: ~1200 pages total

### For Formulas
**Scheid, Volker, Dan Bensky, Andrew Ellis, and Randall Barolet.** *Chinese Herbal Medicine: Formulas and Strategies.* 2nd ed. Seattle: Eastland Press, 2009.
- **Use for**: All formula compositions, modifications, clinical applications
- **Organization**: By therapeutic category
- **Page range**: ~1000 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

**Alternative:**
**Maciocia, Giovanni.** *The Practice of Chinese Medicine: The Treatment of Diseases with Acupuncture and Chinese Herbs.* 2nd ed. Philadelphia: Elsevier Churchill Livingstone, 2007.
- **Use for**: Clinical applications, disease-based approach
- **Organization**: By disease/condition
- **Page range**: ~1300 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

### For Diagnosis
**Maciocia, Giovanni.** *Diagnosis in Chinese Medicine: A Comprehensive Guide.* 2nd ed. Philadelphia: Elsevier Churchill Livingstone, 2018.
- **Use for**: Tongue diagnosis, pulse diagnosis, questioning
- **Organization**: By diagnostic method
- **Tongue section**: pp. 259-380
- **Pulse section**: pp. 381-520
- **Questioning section**: pp. 135-258
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

### For Theory & Patterns
**Maciocia, Giovanni.** *The Foundations of Chinese Medicine: A Comprehensive Text for Acupuncturists and Herbalists.* 3rd ed. Philadelphia: Elsevier Churchill Livingstone, 2015.
- **Use for**: Pattern differentiation, theory, treatment principles
- **Organization**: By topic (theory, diagnosis, patterns, treatment)
- **Page range**: ~1200 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

### For Points
**Deadman, Peter, Mazin Al-Khafaji, and Kevin Baker.** *A Manual of Acupuncture.* 2nd ed. East Sussex, England: Journal of Chinese Medicine Publications, 2007.
- **Use for**: Point locations, functions, indications, combinations
- **Organization**: By channel
- **Page range**: ~700 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes

**Alternative:**
**Cheng, Xinnong, ed.** *Chinese Acupuncture and Moxibustion.* 3rd ed. Beijing: Foreign Languages Press, 2009.
- **Use for**: Point locations, techniques, theory
- **Organization**: By topic
- **Page range**: ~600 pages total
- **NCCAOM Primary Reference**: Yes (CALE uses this as primary)

## Tips for Efficient Extraction

### When Creating Herb Notes:
1. **Look up the herb** in Bensky Materia Medica using the page numbers above
2. **Extract key information**:
   - Properties (taste, temperature, channels entered)
   - Functions (primary and secondary)
   - Indications (what it treats)
   - Dosage (typical range)
   - Cautions/Contraindications
   - Processing methods (if applicable)
3. **Cross-reference** with Chen if needed for pharmacology
4. **Use existing herb notes** as templates for formatting

### When Creating Formula Notes:
1. **Look up the formula** in Scheid/Bensky using the page numbers above
2. **Extract key information**:
   - Chief herbs (Jun)
   - Deputy herbs (Chen)
   - Assistant herbs (Zuo)
   - Envoy herbs (Shi)
   - Functions and indications
   - Tongue and pulse
   - Modifications
   - Cautions/Contraindications
3. **Cross-reference** with Maciocia Practice for clinical applications
4. **Use existing formula notes** as templates for formatting

### When Creating Concept Guides:
1. **Start with Maciocia** (Foundations or Diagnosis depending on topic)
2. **Read the entire section** to understand the concept fully
3. **Create comprehensive notes** with:
   - Definition and theory
   - Clinical manifestations
   - Diagnostic criteria
   - Treatment principles
   - Examples and case studies
4. **Add visual aids** (charts, diagrams, tables) where helpful
