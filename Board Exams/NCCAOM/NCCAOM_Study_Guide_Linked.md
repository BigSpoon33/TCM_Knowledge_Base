## Content Outlines with Links

### Foundations of Oriental Medicine (FOM)

#### Domain I: Clinical Examination Methods (35% of Exam)

##### A. Looking (Wang) - [[Inspection]]

**1. Spirit (Shen)**
- Identify outward manifestation of Spirit (Shen)
  - Complexion, expression, demeanor, general behavior
- Relate state of Spirit to clinical significance

**2. Face, Eyes, Nose, Ears, Mouth, Lips, Teeth, Head, Throat**
- Identify normal and abnormal conditions
- Related symptoms:
  - [[Facial Color Changes]]
  - [[Eye Disorders]]
  - [[Nasal Discharge]]
  - [[Mouth and Lip Disorders]]
  - [[Throat Disorders]]

**3. Tongue Diagnosis**
- Identify normal and abnormal manifestations
- Tongue features to assess:
  - Color: [[Red Tongue]], [[Pale Tongue]], [[Purple Tongue]]
  - Coating: [[Thick Tongue Coating]], [[Thin Tongue Coating]], [[Yellow Tongue Coating]], [[White Tongue Coating]]
  - Size and shape
  - Moisture and texture
  - Position and movement
  - Sublingual veins

**4. Physical Characteristics**
- Form, movement, physical characteristics
  - [[Body Constitution]]
  - Hair, nails, skin
- Skin conditions: [[Skin Disorders]]
- Excretions:
  - [[Sputum]]
  - [[Sweat]]
  - [[Vaginal Discharge]]
  - [[Stool]]
  - [[Urine]]

##### B. Listening and Smelling (Wen) - [[Auscultation and Olfaction]]

**1. Sounds**
- Respiratory sounds
- Voice and speech quality
- Related: [[Cough]], [[Wheezing]], [[Voice Changes]]

**2. Odors**
- Body odors
- Breath/mouth odors
- Excretion/secretion odors

##### C. Asking (Wen) - Patient Interview

**1. Chief Complaint**
- Onset, location, aggravation factors
- Related to pattern differentiation

**2. Current Health - Ten Questions (Shi Wen)**
- [[Pain]]
- [[Energy Level]]
- [[Sweating]]
- [[Appetite]]
- [[Thirst]]
- [[Sleep Disorders]]
- [[Urination]]
- [[Bowel Movements]]
- [[Menstruation]] (for women)
- [[Temperature Sensations]]

**3. Environmental and Social History**
- Internal factors: [[Seven Emotional Factors]]
  - [[Anger]]
  - [[Joy]]
  - [[Worry]]
  - [[Pensiveness]]
  - [[Sadness]]
  - [[Fear]]
  - [[Fright]]
- External factors:
  - [[Wind]]
  - [[Cold]]
  - [[Summer Heat]]
  - [[Damp]]
  - [[Dryness]]
  - [[Fire]]
- Miscellaneous factors:
  - Dietary habits
  - Work habits
  - Occupation

**4. Health History**
- Personal health history
- Family history
- Previous treatments

##### D. Palpation (Qie) - [[Palpation]]

**1. Pulse Diagnosis**
- 28 pulse types (know characteristics, clinical significance)
- Common pulses:
  - Floating (Fu Mai)
  - Deep (Chen Mai)
  - Slow (Chi Mai)
  - Rapid (Shu Mai)
  - Deficient (Xu Mai)
  - Excess (Shi Mai)
  - Wiry (Xuan Mai)
  - Slippery (Hua Mai)
  - Thready (Xi Mai)

**2. Body Palpation**
- Abdomen
- Channels and points
- Masses and nodules
- Temperature
