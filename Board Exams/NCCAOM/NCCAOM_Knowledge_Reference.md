## Overview

The NCCAOM offers certification in:
- **Foundations of Oriental Medicine (FOM)**
- **Biomedicine (BIO)**
- **Acupuncture with Point Location (ACP)**
- **Chinese Herbology (CH)**
- **Oriental Medicine (OM)** - Comprehensive certification including all above

All examinations administered in 2022 are based on content outlines derived from a job analysis conducted in 2017.

---

## Content Outlines Summary

### Foundations of Oriental Medicine

**Domain I: Clinical Examination Methods (35% of Exam)**
- Looking (Wang): Spirit, Face, Tongue, Physical characteristics
- Listening and Smelling (Wen): Sounds, Odors
- Asking (Wen): Chief complaint, Current health, History
- Palpation (Qie): Pulse, Palpation of body

**Domain II: Pattern/Syndrome Differentiation (45% of Exam)**
- Eight Principles
- Qi, Blood, and Body Fluids
- Zang Fu Organs
- Six Stages, Four Levels, San Jiao
- Channels and Collaterals
- Five Elements

**Domain III: Treatment Principles and Strategies (20% of Exam)**
- Identify treatment principles
- Apply treatment strategies
- Determine prognosis

### Biomedicine

**Domain I: Basic Sciences (15% of Exam)**
- Anatomy and Physiology
- Pathophysiology
- Microbiology
- Pharmacology
- Nutrients and Supplements

**Domain II: Clinical Sciences (55% of Exam)**
- Patient assessment
- Diagnostic procedures
- Clinical assessment process

**Domain III: Patient Management (30% of Exam)**
- Patient education
- Referral and collaboration
- Safety and infection control

### Acupuncture with Point Location

**Domain I: Point Location and Functions (50% of Exam)**
- Regular channel points
- Extra points
- Point categories

**Domain II: Treatment Principles and Point Selection (35% of Exam)**
- Point selection strategies
- Point combinations
- Treatment modifications

**Domain III: Needling Techniques and Adjunctive Therapies (15% of Exam)**
- Needling techniques
- Moxibustion
- Cupping, Gua Sha, other modalities

### Chinese Herbology

**Domain I: Materia Medica and Formulas (60% of Exam)**
- Individual herbs
- Chinese herbal formulas
- Preparation and administration

**Domain II: Treatment Principles and Strategies (20% of Exam)**
- Treatment strategies
- Formula modifications
- Herbal pairing

**Domain III: Patient Management (20% of Exam)**
- Patient assessment
- Patient education
- Treatment evaluation
