Actions & Indications 
• Invigorates the blood and unblocks the collaterals: for chest 
pain and hemiplegia secondary to blood stasis. 
-
With Salviae miltiorrhizae Radix (dan shen), Carthami 
Flos (hong hua), and Persicae Semen (tao ren) for chest 
pain and hemiplegia from obstruction due to blood stasis. 
• Clears heat and resolves toxicity: for cough from Lung heat 
or a swollen and painful throat. Also applied topically as a 
powder for burns. 
-
With Scutellariae Radix (huang qin) and Trichosanthis 
Fructus (gua l6u) for cough due to Lung heat. 
-
With Lasiosphaera/Calvatia (rna b6) and Belamcandae 
Rhizoma (she gan) for swollen and painful throat. 
Commentary 
There are no references to Ilicis pubescentis Radix (mao 
dong qzng) in the classical literature, but in recent years a 
significant amount of research has been undertaken on 
the effects of this herb, informing its adoption into main-
stream usage. Acrid, such that it disperses, bitter, such that 
it drains and directs downward, and cold, such that it cools 
heat, this herb primarily invigorates the blood, unblocks 
the collaterals, clears heat, and resolves toxicity. 
The earliest reference to this herb is in a twentieth-
century text describing local herb use in south China, the
