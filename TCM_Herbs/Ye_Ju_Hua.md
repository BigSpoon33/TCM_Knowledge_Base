Commentary 
Bitter, acrid, and slightly cold, Chrysanthemi indici Flos 
(ye ju huii) enters the Lung and Liver channels to drain fire, 
disperse wind, clear heat, resolve toxicity, and reduce swell-
ing. Seeking Accuracy in the Materia Medica observes: 
This is an herb for external medicine's swollen sores. Its 
flavor is acrid and also bitter, with a great ability to dis-
perse fire and disperse qi. Thus, any condition such as 
toxic sores, swollen abscess, scrofula, hot painful eyes, or 
stagnant blood in women will never lack this in its treat-
ment, because acridity can disperse qi, bitterness can dis-
perse fire .... However, those with deficient Stomach qi 
cannot take it recklessly. 
Rectification of the Meaning of Materia Medica elaborates: 
In addition to the other types of chrysanthemum, Chrysan-
themi indici Flos (ye ju huii) grows in most places, the flow-
ers and leaves of which are little, the stamens small and 
bean-like. The color is definitely yellow, and its actions are 
fierce and violent, with a particularly bitter flavor; its leaves 
are slender and tough. It can be used in external medicine 
as a topical application.
