# Edema Flashcards

## Edema
What is the TCM term for edema, and what does it mean literally?
?
*Shui Zhong* (水肿), meaning "water swelling."

**Flashcard 2**

---
## Edema
In TCM, what is the underlying cause of edema (Shui Zhong)?
?
A systemic imbalance disrupting the body's fluid metabolism, affecting the transformation, transportation, and excretion of fluids.

**Flashcard 3**

---
## Edema
Which Zang-Fu organs are most closely involved in the development of edema according to TCM?
?
Spleen (Pi), Kidney (Shen), Lung (Fei), and San Jiao (Triple Burner).

**Flashcard 4**

---
## Edema
How does Spleen Qi deficiency contribute to edema?
?
Impairs the Spleen's ability to transform and transport fluids, leading to Damp accumulation, especially in the lower limbs.

**Flashcard 5**

---
## Edema
What are the key clinical signs of Kidney Yang deficiency-related edema?
?
Lower limb edema (severe, worse in the evening), aversion to cold, frequent nocturia.

**Flashcard 6**

---
## Edema
What role does the Lung play in fluid metabolism, according to TCM?
?
Governs Qi and disperses fluids to the exterior of the body; Lung Qi deficiency can lead to facial and upper body edema.

**Flashcard 7**

---
## Edema
Give an example of an internal factor contributing to edema from a TCM perspective.
?
Excessive consumption of raw, cold, greasy, or sweet foods weakening the Spleen. or Emotional factors such as prolonged worry.

**Flashcard 8**

---
## Edema
What are some clinical features of edema related to Damp-Heat?
?
Shiny, tight, possibly red skin; thirst; scanty dark urine; constipation; feeling of heat.

**Flashcard 9**

---
## Edema
What are the general treatment principles for edema in TCM?
?
Strengthening the Spleen, warming and tonifying the Kidney, promoting Lung Qi, resolving Dampness, promoting Qi circulation, and invigorating blood circulation.

**Flashcard 10**

---
## Edema
From a TCM perspective, what is the role of the San Jiao (Triple Burner) in the development of edema?
?
It is considered the pathway of fluids, and dysfunction of the San Jiao disrupts the flow of fluids throughout the body, contributing to edema.
