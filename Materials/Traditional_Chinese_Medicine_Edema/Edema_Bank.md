# Edema Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is the term for edema?**

**Correct Answer:** Shui Zhong

**Explanation:** In TCM, edema is termed *Shui Zhong* (水肿), which translates to "water swelling."

**Options:**
- Wei Qi
- Xue Yu
- Shui Zhong
- Jing Luo

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following Zang-Fu organs is primarily responsible for the transformation and transportation of fluids, and its deficiency often leads to Damp accumulation and edema, particularly in the lower limbs?**

**Correct Answer:** Spleen (Pi)

**Explanation:** The Spleen governs transformation and transportation of fluids. Spleen Qi deficiency impairs its ability to transform fluids, leading to Damp accumulation and edema, particularly in the lower limbs.

**Options:**
- Kidney (Shen)
- Lung (Fei)
- Spleen (Pi)
- Liver (Gan)

---
### Question 3

**According to TCM, what type of edema is characterized by shiny, tight, and possibly red skin?**

**Correct Answer:** Damp-Heat

**Explanation:** Damp-Heat edema is characterized by shiny, tight, possibly red skin.

**Options:**
- Spleen Qi Deficiency
- Kidney Yang Deficiency
- Lung Qi Deficiency
- Damp-Heat

---
### Question 4

**Which of the following is NOT a key Zang-Fu organ involved in fluid metabolism according to TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Heart

**Explanation:** The key Zang-Fu organs involved in fluid metabolism according to TCM are the Spleen, Kidney, Lung, and San Jiao (Triple Burner).

**Options:**
- Spleen
- Kidney
- Lung
- Heart

---
### Question 5

**Which treatment principle is most appropriate for a patient diagnosed with Spleen Qi deficiency induced edema?**

**Correct Answer:** Strengthening the Spleen

**Explanation:** For Spleen Qi deficiency, the general treatment principle involves strengthening the Spleen by tonifying Spleen Qi to improve its transformation and transportation functions.

**Options:**
- Warming and Tonifying the Kidney
- Promoting Lung Qi
- Strengthening the Spleen
- Invigorating Blood Circulation
