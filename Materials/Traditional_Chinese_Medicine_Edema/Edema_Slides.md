*   **TCM View:** Systemic imbalance affecting fluid metabolism. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Emphasis:** Root cause of organ dysfunction, not just the symptom. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Key Players:** Spleen, Kidney, Lung, San Jiao. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **"Water Passage"** (Ê∞¥ÈÅì): Network for fluid regulation. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: [TCM focuses on the systemic disharmony leading to edema, rather than simply treating the swelling.]

## Etiology (Causes)

::: grid
::: col
### Internal Factors
*   **Dietary Imbalances:** Raw, cold, greasy, sweet foods.
*   **Emotional Factors:** Worry, pensiveness injures Spleen.
*   **Chronic Illness:** Weakens Zang-Fu organs.
*   **Constitutional Weakness:** Predisposition to deficiency.
:::
::: col
### External Factors
*   **Invasion of External Pathogens:**
    *   Wind
    *   Cold
    *   Dampness
    *   Heat
* Wind-Water: facial edema. Damp-Heat: shiny, tight skin.
:::
:::
Note: [Causes of edema range from internal imbalances to external invasions.]

## Clinical Manifestations: Spleen Qi Deficiency

*   **Location:** Lower limbs (pitting edema) <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Skin:** Pale, cool <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Temperature:** Cold <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Sensation:** Heavy, tired limbs <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Accompanying:** Fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools, abdominal distension <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Pale, swollen, teeth marks <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: [Classic Spleen Qi Deficiency presentation.]

---

## Clinical Manifestations: Kidney Yang Deficiency

*   **Location:** Lower limbs (severe pitting, worse evening) <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Skin:** Pale, cold <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Temperature:** Cold <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Sensation:** Heavy, cold sensation, lower back pain <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Accompanying:** Scanty urination, frequent nocturia, aversion to cold <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Pale, swollen, teeth marks <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: [Think Kidney Yang when edema is worse in the evening and patient feels cold.]

---

## Clinical Manifestations: Lung Qi Deficiency & Damp-Heat

::: grid
::: col
### Lung Qi Deficiency
*   **Location:** Facial, upper body
*   **Skin:** Pale
*   **Temperature:** Normal/slightly cool
*   **Sensation:** Tiredness
*   **Accompanying:** SOB, cough, wind aversion
*   **Tongue:** Pale
:::
::: col
### Damp-Heat
*   **Location:** Lower limbs, generalized
*   **Skin:** Shiny, tight, possibly red
*   **Temperature:** Warm/hot
*   **Sensation:** Heavy, possibly painful
*   **Accompanying:** Thirst, scanty dark urine, constipation, heat
*   **Tongue:** Red with yellow greasy coat
:::
:::

Note: [Lung = Upper body edema, Damp-Heat = Shiny, tight skin.]
