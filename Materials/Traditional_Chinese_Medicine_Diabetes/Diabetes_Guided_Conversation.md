## 3. Etiology & Pathomechanisms

**Question:**
What causes Etiology & Pathomechanisms and how does it develop?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
**Primary Causes (Etiology):**
*   **Dietary Impropriety (飲食不節):** Excessive consumption of rich, greasy, or sweet foods generates internal heat and dampness, overburdening the Stomach and impairing its function of transforming and transporting fluids. This can lead to Yin deficiency over time.
*   Example: Regularly eating deep-fried foods and sugary desserts.
*   **Emotional Stress (情志失调):** Prolonged emotional stress, such as anger, frustration, worry, or overthinking, can disrupt the flow of Qi, particularly affecting the Liver. Liver Qi stagnation can generate heat, further depleting Yin.
*   Example: Chronic work-related stress or unresolved grief.

## 5. Treatment Principles

**Question:**
What are the main treatment principles for Treatment Principles?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
**Treatment Principles:**
*   **Nourish Yin and Generate Fluids:** Replenish depleted Yin to moisten dryness and quench thirst, a primary strategy, especially for Lower Jiao patterns.
*   **Clear Heat:** Reduce internal heat that contributes to the consumption of fluids, often targeting Stomach and Lung heat.
*   **Tonify Qi:** Strengthen Qi to improve the transportation and transformation of fluids and support organ function.
*   **Promote Fluid Metabolism:** Regulate the movement and distribution of fluids throughout the body, addressing edema or urinary dysfunction.

*   **Key Elements:** Excessive thirst, increased hunger, frequent urination, and a weakening of the body.
*   **Analogy:** Think of a plant wilting due to lack of water (Yin deficiency). Despite watering it (drinking fluids), the water is not being properly retained and distributed.
