# Diabetes Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is Diabetes Mellitus known as?**

**Correct Answer:** Xiaoke Bing

**Explanation:** In TCM, Diabetes Mellitus is known as *Xiaoke Bing*, which translates to 'Wasting and Thirsting Disease.'

**Options:**
- Shanghan Lun
- Huangdi Neijing
- Xiaoke Bing
- Jinkui Yaolue

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following is NOT considered a key organ in the TCM understanding of Xiaoke?**

**Correct Answer:** Liver

**Explanation:** The key organs in the TCM understanding of Xiaoke are the Lungs, Stomach, and Kidneys. They play vital roles in fluid metabolism and overall balance.

**Options:**
- Lungs
- Stomach
- Kidneys
- Liver

---
### Question 3

**According to TCM, what is a primary underlying factor in Xiaoke?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidney Yin deficiency

**Explanation:** Kidney Yin deficiency is considered a primary underlying factor in Xiaoke. It leads to a failure to nourish and moisten the body, resulting in dryness and excessive urination.

**Options:**
- Lung Yang excess
- Kidney Yin deficiency
- Stomach Qi excess
- Blood excess

---
### Question 4

**Which Jiao (burner) is associated with excessive thirst as a dominant symptom?**

**Correct Answer:** Upper Jiao

**Explanation:** The Upper Jiao (Lung) is associated with excessive thirst as a dominant symptom, including dry mouth and throat, and a preference for cold drinks.

**Options:**
- Lower Jiao
- Middle Jiao
- Upper Jiao
- All Jiao

---
### Question 5

**What is the dominant symptom associated with the Middle Jiao in the context of Xiaoke?**

**Correct Answer:** Excessive hunger

**Explanation:** The Middle Jiao (Stomach) is associated with excessive hunger, eating a lot but still losing weight. This indicates an imbalance affecting the stomach's digestive and transformative functions.

**Options:**
- Excessive urination
- Excessive hunger
- Excessive thirst
- Weakness in lower back
