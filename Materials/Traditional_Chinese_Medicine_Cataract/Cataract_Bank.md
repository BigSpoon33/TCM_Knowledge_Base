# Cataract Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is the term for cataracts?**

**Correct Answer:** Nei Zhang

**Explanation:** In TCM, cataracts are referred to as "Nei Zhang" (内障), which signifies an "inner obstruction" or "inner barrier" affecting vision.

**Options:**
- Bai Nei
- Wai Zhang
- Nei Zhang
- Mu Bing

### Question 2

**According to the Five Wheels Theory (Wu Lun Xue Shuo), which Zang organ is associated with the Black Wheel (pupil)?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidney

**Explanation:** The Black Wheel (瞳神轮), which is the pupil, is specifically associated with the Kidney in the Five Wheels Theory.

**Options:**
- Liver
- Kidney
- Spleen
- Heart

---
### Question 3

**Which of the following is NOT a common clinical manifestation of cataracts according to TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Increased night vision

**Explanation:** Common visual disturbances associated with cataracts, as understood by TCM, include blurred vision, glare, decreased night vision, double vision, and color distortion. Increased night vision is not a symptom.

**Options:**
- Blurred vision
- Increased night vision
- Glare
- Color distortion

### Question 4

**Which herbal formula is commonly used in TCM for Liver and Kidney Yin deficiency, especially in the early stages of cataracts?**

**Correct Answer:** Qi Ju Di Huang Wan

**Explanation:** Qi Ju Di Huang Wan (杞菊地黄丸) is a classic formula for Liver and Kidney Yin deficiency, often used in the early stages of cataracts in TCM.

**Options:**
- Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang
- Qi Ju Di Huang Wan
- Xiao Yao San

### Question 5

**What is the primary goal of TCM treatment for cataracts?**

**Correct Answer:** To correct the underlying imbalances causing the cataract

**Explanation:** TCM treatment aims to address the root cause of the cataract by correcting the underlying imbalances within the body's systems. While it may not always reverse advanced cataracts, it can slow progression and alleviate symptoms.

**Options:**
- To reverse advanced cataracts completely
- To eliminate the need for surgery in all cases
- To correct the underlying imbalances causing the cataract
- To only address the symptoms of the cataract
