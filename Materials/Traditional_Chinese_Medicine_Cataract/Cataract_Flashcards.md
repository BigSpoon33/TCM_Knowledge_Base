# Cataract Flashcards

## Cataract
What is the TCM term for cataracts and what does it signify?
?
*Nei Zhang* (内障), meaning "inner obstruction" or "inner barrier" affecting vision.

## Cataract
According to TCM, what organ systems are most commonly associated with the development of cataracts?
?
The Liver and Kidney systems.

## Cataract
What is the Five Wheels Theory (Wu Lun Xue Shuo) and how does it relate to cataracts?
?
It maps parts of the eye to the Five Zang organs.  The Black Wheel (pupil) is associated with the Kidney, which is most directly affected. The Wind Wheel (iris) is associated with the Liver, also crucial for vision.

## Cataract
Name three common visual disturbances associated with cataracts according to TCM.
?
Blurred vision, glare, decreased night vision, double vision, or color distortion. (Any three are acceptable)

## Cataract
Name three common acupuncture points or point categories used in TCM treatment of cataracts.
?
Jingming, Zanzhu, Sizhukong (points around the eyes), Taichong, Taixi (distal Liver/Kidney points), Zusanli, Sanyinjiao (points to tonify Qi and Blood).

## Cataract
What is the TCM perspective on the role of surgery in cataract treatment?
?
Surgery is the definitive treatment for significant visual impairment, and TCM can be used as a complementary therapy to support recovery, improve eye health, and prevent recurrence by addressing underlying imbalances.
