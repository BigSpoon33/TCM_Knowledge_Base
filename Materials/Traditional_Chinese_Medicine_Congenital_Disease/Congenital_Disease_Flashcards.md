# Congenital Disease Flashcards

## Congenital Disease
What is the TCM definition of congenital diseases?
?
Conditions present at birth, stemming from deficiencies in *Jing* (especially Kidney *Jing*) inherited from the parents or from disruptions during gestation.

## Congenital Disease
According to the *Huangdi Neijing*, what is the importance of prenatal care?
?
The mother's health and emotional state during pregnancy are crucial for the healthy development of the fetus. Maternal imbalances can weaken the fetus, predisposing the child to illness.

## Congenital Disease
List three key prenatal influences, according to TCM, that can impact the fetus.
?
Mother's Health, Mother's Emotions, and Environment (Exposure to Pathogens or Toxins), Paternal Influence.

## Congenital Disease
What is the potential consequence of prolonged stress or anxiety in the mother during pregnancy, according to TCM?
?
Disrupted *Qi* and Blood flow, affecting fetal development and potentially leading to emotional or physical imbalances in the child. Liver Qi Stagnation.

## Congenital Disease
In TCM, what are the Cardinal Symptoms of congenital diseases?
?
Failure to Thrive, Frequent Illnesses, Developmental Delays, Weak Constitution, Congenital Defects.

## Congenital Disease
What are the common symptoms of Delayed Motor Skills due to Kidney Jing Deficiency and how it is diagnosed through TCM diagnosis?
?
Weak limbs, slow development, delayed teething, poor bone growth. Pale, thin, possibly tooth-marked tongue and weak, deep pulse, especially at the Kidney position.

A child has frequent respiratory infections and slow development. What TCM pattern might be suspected?

?
Poor muscle tone, fatigue, weak digestion, delayed growth. Pale, swollen, possibly with a thin white coating tongue and weak pulse, especially at the Spleen position.
