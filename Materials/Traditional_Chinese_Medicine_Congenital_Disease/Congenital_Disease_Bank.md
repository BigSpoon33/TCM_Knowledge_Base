# Congenital Disease Question Bank

### Question 1

**According to TCM, what is the primary origin of congenital diseases?**

**Correct Answer:** Imbalances established during prenatal development

**Explanation:** TCM views congenital diseases as stemming from imbalances in Jing and Qi established during prenatal development, rather than solely focusing on genetics or post-natal environmental factors.

**Options:**
- Genetic mutations
- Environmental toxins encountered post-birth
- Imbalances established during prenatal development
- Infections contracted shortly after birth

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following is considered a key influence on fetal development, according to TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** The mother's emotional state during pregnancy

**Explanation:** TCM emphasizes the importance of the mother's emotional state, as prolonged stress, anxiety, fear, or anger can disrupt Qi and Blood flow, negatively affecting fetal development.

**Options:**
- The father's career
- The mother's emotional state during pregnancy
- The amount of sunlight the mother receives
- The type of music the mother listens to

### Question 3

**What is *Jing* in the context of congenital diseases in TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** A vital substance passed down from parents

**Explanation:** *Jing* is considered the essence passed down from parents, particularly Kidney *Jing*, and its deficiency is often implicated in congenital conditions.

**Options:**
- A type of acupuncture needle
- A specific herbal formula
- A vital substance passed down from parents
- A method of massage

### Question 5

**Which of the following is a cardinal symptom of congenital diseases, as described by TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Failure to thrive

**Explanation:** Failure to thrive (poor growth and weight gain) is a key clinical manifestation reflecting a fundamental weakness in Jing and Qi, common in children with congenital diseases.

**Options:**
- Excessive appetite
- Failure to thrive
- Insomnia
- Unexplained weight gain
