# Cramping Question Bank

### Question 1

**According to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is the term for cramping?**

**Correct Answer:** Jìng Luán (痙攣)

**Explanation:** In TCM, cramping is referred to as Jìng Luán (痙攣). The other options represent other TCM concepts: Bi Syndrome, Pain, and Qi Stagnation respectively.

**Options:**
- Bi Zheng (痹證)
- Tòng (痛)
- Jìng Luán (痙攣)
- Qi Zhi (氣滯)

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following is NOT considered a potential cause of cramping in TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Yin Excess

**Explanation:** TCM attributes cramping to Qi Stagnation, Blood Stasis, Cold Agglutination, Damp Obstruction, or deficiencies of Qi, Blood, Yin, or Yang. Yin Excess is not listed as a direct cause.

**Options:**
- Qi Stagnation
- Blood Stasis
- Yin Excess
- Cold Agglutination

---
### Question 3

**The TCM principle 'Bù tōng zé tòng' translates to:**

**Correct Answer:** No free flow, then there is pain.

**Explanation:** 'Bù tōng zé tòng' (不通則痛) means 'no free flow, then there is pain', encapsulating the TCM understanding that pain arises from blockages in the flow of Qi and Blood.

**Options:**
- Wind causes joint pain.
- No free flow, then there is pain.
- Dampness leads to stiffness.
- Heat clears stagnation.

---
### Question 4

**Which of the following is an alleviating factor for abdominal cramping according to TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Heat

**Explanation:** According to the provided text, applying heat to the abdomen is an alleviating factor for abdominal cramping in TCM. Other alleviating factors include gentle massage, bowel movements, and relaxation.

**Options:**
- Cold exposure
- Pressure on the abdomen
- Heat
- Emotional stress

### Question 5

**In TCM, spasm is considered:**

**Correct Answer:** A severe form of constraint, signifying a significant blockage of Qi and Blood.

**Explanation:** The text states that spasm is considered a more extreme form of constraint, indicating a severe blockage of Qi and Blood, compelling the muscle into forceful, involuntary contraction.

**Options:**
- A mild form of Qi stagnation.
- A type of Bi Syndrome.
- A severe form of constraint, signifying a significant blockage of Qi and Blood.
- An imbalance of Yin and Yang.
