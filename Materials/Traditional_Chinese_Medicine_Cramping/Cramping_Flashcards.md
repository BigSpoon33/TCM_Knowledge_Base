# Cramping Flashcards

## Cramping
How is cramping defined in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)?
?
Cramping (*jìng luán*) is a sudden, involuntary, and often painful muscular contraction or spasm, often categorized under *bi* syndrome, indicating a disruption in the smooth flow of Qi and Blood.

## Cramping
What is the TCM principle regarding pain, and how does it relate to cramping?
?
The principle "不通則痛 – *bù tōng zé tòng*" means "no free flow, then there is pain." Cramping is seen as a manifestation of blocked Qi and Blood flow, leading to pain.

## Cramping
Name five potential causes of Qi and Blood blockage that can lead to cramping in TCM.
?
Qi Stagnation, Blood Stasis, Cold Agglutination, Damp Obstruction, and Deficiency (of Qi, Blood, Yin, or Yang).

## Cramping
How does Cold Agglutination contribute to cramping?
?
Exposure to Cold can cause contraction and impede circulation, leading to cramping. This is often seen in abdominal cramping after consuming cold foods or drinks.

## Cramping
What is Bi Syndrome (*bì zhèng*) in TCM, and how does it relate to cramping?
?
Bi Syndrome is a general category encompassing painful obstructions, often triggered by external pathogenic factors like Wind, Cold, Damp, or Heat. Cramping can fall under *bi* syndrome when caused by these external factors.

## Cramping
In TCM, what does it mean when someone experiences a spasm?
?
Spasm (*chōu chù*, *jū luán*) is considered a more severe form of constraint, signifying a significant blockage of Qi and Blood, forcing the muscle into an uncontrolled contraction.

## Cramping
List three common locations for cramping and their associated pain quality, according to TCM.
?
Abdominal (sharp, cramping, colicky), Leg (sharp, stabbing, tight), Hand (sharp, cramping, aching).

## Cramping
What are some common aggravating factors for abdominal cramping, according to TCM?
?
Cold exposure, pressure on the abdomen, certain foods (cold, raw, greasy), and emotional stress.

**Flashcard 9**

---
## Cramping
What are some common alleviating factors for leg cramps, according to TCM?
?
Heat, gentle massage, stretching, and hydration.

## Cramping
Give two associated symptoms that can accompany abdominal cramping.
?
Nausea and vomiting, diarrhea or constipation, excessive gas and bloating, irritability or mood changes.
