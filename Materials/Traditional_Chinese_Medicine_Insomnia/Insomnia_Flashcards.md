# Insomnia Flashcards

## Insomnia
In TCM, what is the primary reason for insomnia?
?
Insomnia is caused by an underlying disharmony within the body's energetic systems, preventing the *Shen* (Spirit/Mind) from being properly housed and anchored.

Which organ system is most closely associated with the *Shen* in TCM?
?
The Heart (*Xin*).

## Insomnia
What is the role of *Yin* and Blood in relation to sleep, according to TCM?
?
The *Shen* needs to descend and be rooted in *Yin* and Blood for sleep to occur. *Yin* anchors the *Yang* and nourishes the *Shen*.

List three organ systems (other than the heart) commonly involved in TCM patterns of insomnia.
?
Spleen, Kidney, and Liver.

## Insomnia
What is Heart Blood Deficiency, and how does it contribute to insomnia?
?
Heart Blood Deficiency is an insufficient amount of Blood to nourish the *Shen*. This leads to restlessness, anxiety, and difficulty falling asleep because the *Shen* is unanchored.

## Insomnia
What is Kidney Yin Deficiency and how does it contribute to insomnia?
?
Kidney Yin Deficiency is an inadequate amount of Kidney *Yin* leading to ascending Empty-Heat that disturbs the Heart and *Shen*, causing insomnia, especially difficulty staying asleep.

## Insomnia
What are the symptoms of Liver Qi Stagnation and how does it contribute to insomnia?
?
Symptoms include irritability, distension in the chest and hypochondria, and a wiry pulse. Stagnant Liver *Qi* can transform into Fire, agitating the *Shen* and causing difficulty falling asleep.

## Insomnia
How do TCM practitioners differentiate insomnia from normal sleep variations?
?
By assessing the quality of sleep, its impact on daily functioning, and identifying accompanying symptoms, tongue and pulse diagnosis, duration and severity.
