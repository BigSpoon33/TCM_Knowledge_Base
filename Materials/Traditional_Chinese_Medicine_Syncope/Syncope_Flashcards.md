# Syncope Flashcards

## Syncope
In TCM, what are the fundamental mechanisms involved in the pathogenesis of syncope?
?
Disruption of the normal ascending of clear Yang Qi to nourish the head and the descending of turbid Yin. This disruption can be due to deficiency, stagnation/obstruction, or rebellious Qi.

## Syncope
What are the key symptoms associated with Syncope due to Dampness Obstructing the Middle Jiao?
?
Dizziness, heavy-headedness, nausea, vomiting, chest oppression, poor appetite, fatigue, feeling of fullness in the abdomen, swollen tongue with sticky white coating, slippery soggy pulse.

## Syncope
How does "True *Jue*" differ from "False *Jue*" in the context of Syncope in TCM?
?
True *Jue* involves a complete collapse of Yang Qi, often with cold limbs. False *Jue* may involve underlying Heat signs.

## Syncope
What is the main difference, as stated in this content, between syncope and Epilepsy?
?
Epilepsy typically involves convulsions and may have a prodrome of specific sensory aura. Syncope is usually more abrupt and related to postural changes or triggers.
