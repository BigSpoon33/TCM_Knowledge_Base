# Syncope Question Bank

### Question 1

**Which of the following symptoms is MOST associated with Dampness Obstructing the Middle Jiao as a cause of syncope?**

**Correct Answer:** Feeling of fullness in the abdomen and nausea

**Explanation:** Dampness Obstructing the Middle Jiao is characterized by symptoms such as dizziness, heavy-headedness, nausea, vomiting, chest oppression, poor appetite, and a feeling of fullness in the abdomen. Syncope may occur after eating greasy foods.

**Options:**
- Headache and tinnitus
- Expectoration of thick, yellow phlegm
- Feeling of fullness in the abdomen and nausea
- Sudden onset syncope

### Question 2

**In TCM, syncope caused by Liver Yang Rising is most likely to occur during which of the following circumstances?**

**Correct Answer:** During periods of stress or anger

**Explanation:** Syncope associated with Liver Yang Rising typically occurs during periods of stress or anger, often accompanied by symptoms such as headache, dizziness, tinnitus, irritability, and a flushed face.

**Options:**
- After consuming excessive greasy foods
- During periods of stress or anger
- In humid weather conditions
- After prolonged physical exertion

### Question 3

**Which of the following best describes a TRUE *Jue* according to the provided text?**

**Correct Answer:** Involves a complete collapse of Yang Qi, often with cold limbs.

**Explanation:** True *Jue* involves a complete collapse of Yang Qi, often presenting with cold limbs. This is a crucial distinction from False *Jue*, which may involve underlying Heat signs.

**Options:**
- Involves underlying Heat signs.
- Involves a complete collapse of Yang Qi, often with cold limbs.
- Is typically related to postural changes or triggers.
- Is difficult to distinguish from epilepsy.

### Question 4

**Which of the following acupuncture points is commonly used to tonify Qi and Blood in the treatment of syncope?**

**Correct Answer:** ST 36 (Zusanli)

**Explanation:** ST 36 (Zusanli) is a general point that tonifies Qi and Blood and strengthens the Spleen, making it a common choice for treating syncope related to deficiency.

**Options:**
- LV 2 (Xingjian)
- ST 40 (Fenglong)
- ST 36 (Zusanli)
- GB 20 (Fengchi)

### Question 5

**Which of the following herbal formulas is MOST likely to be used for syncope due to Qi Deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang

**Explanation:** *Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang* (Tonify the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction) is specifically indicated for Qi Deficiency, which is one of the root causes of syncope in TCM.

**Options:**
- Xiao Yao San
- Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- Tian Ma Gou Teng Yin
- Qing Qi Hua Tan Wan
