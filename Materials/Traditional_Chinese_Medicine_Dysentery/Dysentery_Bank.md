# Dysentery Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what are the terms used to describe dysentery?**

**Correct Answer:** Li Ji (痢疾) and Chang Bi (肠癖)

**Explanation:** According to the text, dysentery is known as *Li Ji* (痢疾) or *Chang Bi* (肠癖) in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM).

**Options:**
- Wei Qi and Xue Yu
- Qi stagnation and Blood stasis
- Li Ji (痢疾) and Chang Bi (肠癖)
- Yin Xu and Yang Sheng

### Question 2

**Which of the following is NOT a primary TCM pattern of dysentery described in the provided text?**

**Correct Answer:** Wind-Cold Dysentery

**Explanation:** The text mentions Damp-Heat Dysentery, Toxic Heat Dysentery, Cold-Damp Dysentery and Deficiency-Cold Dysentery as primary patterns, but not Wind-Cold Dysentery.

**Options:**
- Damp-Heat Dysentery (湿热痢)
- Toxic Heat Dysentery (疫毒痢)
- Wind-Cold Dysentery
- Deficiency-Cold Dysentery (虚寒痢)

### Question 3

**Which pattern of dysentery is often associated with Spleen and/or Kidney Yang deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Deficiency-Cold Dysentery (虚寒痢)

**Explanation:** Deficiency-Cold Dysentery is described as a chronic and recurring form arising from Spleen and/or Kidney Yang deficiency.

**Options:**
- Damp-Heat Dysentery (湿热痢)
- Toxic Heat Dysentery (疫毒痢)
- Cold-Damp Dysentery (寒湿痢)
- Deficiency-Cold Dysentery (虚寒痢)

### Question 4

**Which of the following is a characteristic sign of Damp-Heat Dysentery?**

**Correct Answer:** Frequent, urgent bowel movements; Stool with blood and mucus; Red or yellow in color

**Explanation:** The text states that Damp-Heat Dysentery is characterized by frequent, urgent bowel movements, stool with blood and mucus, red or yellow stool color, and a burning sensation in the anus.

**Options:**
- Stool with white mucus
- Abdominal pain relieved by warmth
- Frequent, urgent bowel movements; Stool with blood and mucus; Red or yellow in color
- Thin and watery stool

### Question 5

**According to TCM, what are some of the etiologies contributing to Deficiency-Cold Dysentery?**

**Correct Answer:** Chronic illness; Improper diet; Overexertion; Emotional stress weakening the Spleen and Kidney

**Explanation:** The text indicates that the etiology of Deficiency-Cold Dysentery includes chronic illness, improper diet, overexertion, and emotional stress weakening the Spleen and Kidney.

**Options:**
- Exposure to cold and damp environments
- Highly virulent pathogens
- Consumption of contaminated food or water
- Chronic illness; Improper diet; Overexertion; Emotional stress weakening the Spleen and Kidney
