# Dysentery Flashcards

## Dysentery
What are the TCM names for dysentery?
?
*Li Ji* (痢疾) and *Chang Bi* (肠癖)

**Flashcard 2**

---
## Dysentery
How is dysentery characterized in TCM?
?
Frequent bowel movements containing mucus, pus, and/or blood, accompanied by abdominal pain and tenesmus.

**Flashcard 3**

---
## Dysentery
Which organ is primarily affected by dysentery according to TCM?
?
The Large Intestine.

**Flashcard 4**

---
## Dysentery
Name four primary TCM patterns of dysentery.
?
Damp-Heat Dysentery, Toxic Heat Dysentery, Cold-Damp Dysentery, Deficiency-Cold Dysentery.

**Flashcard 5**

---
## Dysentery
What are some typical stool characteristics of Damp-Heat Dysentery?
?
Stool with blood and mucus, red or yellow in color, offensive odor, burning sensation in the anus.

**Flashcard 6**

---
## Dysentery
What are the key stool characteristics of Toxic Heat Dysentery?
?
Profuse blood and pus in stool, dark red or purplish color, extremely foul odor.

**Flashcard 7**

---
## Dysentery
What are some characteristic signs of Cold-Damp Dysentery?
?
Stool with white mucus, abdominal pain relieved by warmth, lack of thirst, dull complexion.

**Flashcard 8**

---
## Dysentery
What are the main stool characteristics for Deficiency-Cold Dysentery?
?
Intermittent dysentery with pus and blood in the stool, thin and watery stool.

**Flashcard 9**

---
## Dysentery
What systemic signs might be observed in a patient with Damp-Heat Dysentery?
?
Possible fever, thirst, irritability, yellowish tongue coating, rapid pulse.

**Flashcard 10**

---
## Dysentery
What are some common causes contributing to Deficiency-Cold Dysentery?
?
Chronic illness, improper diet, overexertion, emotional stress weakening the Spleen and Kidney.
