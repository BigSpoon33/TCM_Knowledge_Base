## Kidney Yin Deficiency
*Nourishing the Cooling Fluids*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Scanty/absent menstruation <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Hot flashes, night sweats <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Dry skin, dizziness <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Red, peeled, possibly with cracks <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Pulse:** Thin, rapid <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Treatment Principle:** Nourish Kidney Yin, Clear Deficiency Heat <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Example Formula:** *Liu Wei Di Huang Wan* <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: Focus on symptoms related to heat and dryness.  Remember *Liu Wei Di Huang Wan* is a classic formula.

---
## Kidney Yang Deficiency
*Warming and Activating the Energy*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Late menstruation, light flow <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Cold limbs, low back pain <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Frequent urination, fatigue <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Pale, swollen <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Pulse:** Deep, weak, slow <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Treatment Principle:** Warm and tonify Kidney Yang <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Example Formula:** *You Gui Wan* <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: Key symptoms are coldness and fatigue. Consider warming acupuncture points and dietary recommendations.

---
## Liver Qi Stagnation
*Soothing the Liver and Regulating Qi*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Irregular cycles, PMS <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Breast tenderness, irritability <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Depression, abdominal distension <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Normal or slightly red on the sides <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Pulse:** Wiry <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Treatment Principle:** Soothe the Liver, Regulate Qi <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Example Formula:** *Xiao Yao Wan* <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: Stress and emotional factors are key. Consider acupuncture points that move Qi.

---
## Blood Deficiency
*Nourishing and Tonifying the Blood*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Pale complexion, dizziness <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Fatigue, scanty/absent menstruation <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Lightheadedness, palpitations <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Pale <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Pulse:** Thin, weak <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Treatment Principle:** Nourish and Tonify Blood <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Example Formula:** *Si Wu Tang* <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: Remember the pale appearance and fatigue. Dietary changes are important in addition to herbs.

## Phlegm Dampness
*Resolving Phlegm and Drying Dampness*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Obesity, irregular menstruation <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Amenorrhea, vaginal discharge <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Edema, fatigue <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Tongue:** Swollen, greasy coating <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Pulse:** Slippery <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Treatment Principle:** Resolve Phlegm, Dry Dampness <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Example Formula:** *Cang Fu Dao Tan Wan* <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: Weight management is crucial.

## Damp Heat
*Clearing Heat, Drying Dampness, Resolving Toxicity*

*   **Signs & Symptoms:**
    *   Yellow, foul-smelling vaginal discharge <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

*   **Treatment Principle:** Clear Heat, Dry Dampness, Resolve Toxicity <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
