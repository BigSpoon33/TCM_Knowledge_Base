# palpitations Flashcards

## palpitations
In TCM, what are palpitations (*xīn jì*) characterized by?
?
An uneasy sensation or awareness of the heartbeat, often accompanied by anxiety, restlessness, shortness of breath, dizziness, and insomnia. It emphasizes the subjective feeling.

## palpitations
What is the key difference in the TCM approach to palpitations compared to Western medicine?
?
TCM focuses on identifying the *pattern* of imbalance manifesting as palpitations and addressing the root cause, whereas Western medicine focuses on identifying the underlying cardiovascular condition.

## palpitations
According to TCM, what role does the Heart play, and how does this relate to palpitations?
?
The Heart is the "Emperor" that governs the mind (Shen). Palpitations are often linked to disturbances of the Shen.

## palpitations
Name three common TCM patterns associated with palpitations.
?
Heart Qi Deficiency, Heart Blood Deficiency, and Phlegm-Fire Disturbing the Heart. (Blood Stasis Obstructing the Heart Vessels, Heart Yin Deficiency also acceptable.)

## palpitations
What are the key symptoms of Heart Qi Deficiency-related palpitations?
?
Palpitations (worse with exertion), shortness of breath, fatigue, spontaneous sweating, pale complexion, dislike of speaking.

## palpitations
What are the key symptoms of Heart Blood Deficiency-related palpitations?

Palpitations, dizziness, insomnia, poor memory, pale complexion, anxiety.
