# palpitations Question Bank

### Question 1

**In TCM, what is the primary focus when addressing palpitations?**

**Correct Answer:** Identifying and addressing the underlying pattern of imbalance causing the palpitations.

**Explanation:** TCM focuses on identifying the 'pattern' of imbalance that is manifesting as palpitations, addressing the root cause of the disharmony rather than just the symptom itself.

**Options:**
- Identifying the specific cardiovascular condition causing the palpitations.
- Slowing down the heart rate to a normal range.
- Identifying and addressing the underlying pattern of imbalance causing the palpitations.
- Administering medications to regulate heart rhythm.

### Question 2

**Which of the following is a key characteristic of Heart Qi Deficiency palpitations?**

**Correct Answer:** Palpitations that worsen with exertion and spontaneous sweating.

**Explanation:** Heart Qi Deficiency palpitations are characterized by palpitations that worsen with exertion, shortness of breath, fatigue, spontaneous sweating, pale complexion, and a dislike of speaking.

**Options:**
- Night sweats and a feeling of heat in the evening.
- Palpitations that worsen with exertion and spontaneous sweating.
- Chest pain that radiates to the left arm.
- Insomnia with vivid dreams and expectoration of phlegm.

### Question 3

**According to TCM, what is the role of the Heart?**

**Correct Answer:** To govern the mind (Shen).

**Explanation:** TCM considers the Heart's role as the 'Emperor' that governs the mind (Shen), so palpitations are often linked to disturbances of the Shen.

**Options:**
- To pump blood throughout the body.
- To filter toxins from the blood.
- To govern the mind (Shen).
- To regulate digestion.

### Question 4

**A patient presents with palpitations, anxiety, insomnia, and a red tongue with little or no coating. Which TCM pattern is most likely?**

**Correct Answer:** Heart Yin Deficiency

**Explanation:** Heart Yin Deficiency is characterized by palpitations, anxiety, insomnia, night sweats, malar flush, dry mouth, and a feeling of heat in the evening. The tongue is typically red, peeled, or cracked, with little or no coating.

**Options:**
- Heart Blood Deficiency
- Heart Qi Deficiency
- Heart Yin Deficiency
- Blood Stasis Obstructing the Heart Vessels

### Question 5

**Which of the following is a common etiology (cause) of Blood Stasis Obstructing the Heart Vessels?**

**Correct Answer:** Trauma, surgery, or prolonged Qi stagnation.

**Explanation:** Common causes of Blood Stasis Obstructing the Heart Vessels include trauma, surgery, prolonged Qi stagnation, and cold invasion.

**Options:**
- Excessive consumption of cold foods.
- Chronic illness and overthinking.
- Trauma, surgery, or prolonged Qi stagnation.
- Overexertion and poor diet.
