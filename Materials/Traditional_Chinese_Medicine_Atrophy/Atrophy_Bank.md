# Atrophy Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is the primary meaning of 'Wei' in the context of Wei Zheng (Atrophy Syndrome)?**

**Correct Answer:** Wilting or Weakening

**Explanation:** The term 'Wei' signifies a wilting or weakening, aptly describing the decline in muscular function associated with Atrophy Syndrome.

**Options:**
- Inflammation
- Pain
- Wilting or Weakening
- Stiffness

### Question 2

**Which of the following is NOT a key factor contributing to the development of Wei Zheng according to TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Excess of Jing

**Explanation:** TCM identifies deficiency of Jing as a contributing factor, not an excess.  The other options are all listed as contributing factors.

**Options:**
- Deficiency of Qi and Blood
- Liver and Kidney Deficiency
- Excess of Jing
- Damp-Heat Obstruction

### Question 3

**A patient presents with weakness primarily in the lower limbs, lower back pain, dizziness, and tinnitus. According to TCM pattern differentiation, which type of Wei Zheng is most likely?**

**Correct Answer:** Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei

**Explanation:** Weakness in the lower limbs, lower back pain, dizziness, and tinnitus are key symptoms of Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei.

**Options:**
- Lung Heat Wei
- Spleen & Stomach Deficiency Wei
- Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei
- Damp-Heat Wei

### Question 4

**Which of the following herbal formulas is commonly used in TCM to address Spleen Qi deficiency in the treatment of Wei Zheng?**

**Correct Answer:** Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang

**Explanation:** Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang (Tonify the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction) is specifically used for Spleen Qi deficiency.

**Options:**
- Liu Wei Di Huang Wan
- Er Miao San
- Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- Gui Pi Tang

---
### Question 5

**In TCM, what is the primary focus of treatment for Wei Zheng?**

**Correct Answer:** Addressing the underlying imbalances and promoting the flow of Qi and Blood

**Explanation:** TCM treatment for Wei Zheng focuses on addressing the underlying imbalances (e.g., Qi and Blood deficiency, Damp-Heat obstruction) and promoting the flow of Qi and Blood to the affected muscles.

**Options:**
- Directly stimulating muscle growth
- Addressing the underlying imbalances and promoting the flow of Qi and Blood
- Blocking pain signals from the brain
- Suppressing the immune system to reduce inflammation
