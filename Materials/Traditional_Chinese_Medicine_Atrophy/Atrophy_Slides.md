## What is Wei Zheng (ÁóøËØÅ)?

*   Literally "Wilting Syndrome"
*   Characterized by:
    *   Progressive muscle weakness <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Muscle atrophy <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
    *   Potential paralysis <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   TCM views it as an imbalance within the body's system

Note: [Wei Zheng, meaning "Wilting Syndrome", is characterized by muscle weakness, atrophy, and possible paralysis. TCM views this as an internal imbalance.]

## Etiology: Key Factors üåø

*   **Qi & Blood Deficiency:** Spleen & Stomach dysfunction
    *   *Example:* Poor diet, digestive issues <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Liver & Kidney Deficiency:** Essence deficiency
    *   *Example:* Age-related decline, lower limb weakness <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Damp-Heat Obstruction:** Environmental and dietary factors
    *   *Example:* Exposure to dampness, spicy foods <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Phlegm Obstruction:** Spleen deficiency, fluid metabolism
    *   *Example:* Chronic cough, mucus production <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
*   **Trauma:** Physical injury
    *   *Example:* Spinal cord injury, localized atrophy <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

Note: [This slide highlights the main causes of Wei Zheng from a TCM perspective, including Qi & Blood deficiency, Liver & Kidney Deficiency, Damp-Heat obstruction, Phlegm Obstruction and Trauma. Each bullet has examples of conditions that may cause these deficiencies.]

## Pattern Differentiation: Lung Heat Wei

| Characteristic      | Description                               |
| :------------------ | :---------------------------------------- |
| **Key Symptoms**   | Upper limb weakness, fever, dry cough     |
| **Tongue**          | Red with thin yellow coating               |
| **Pulse**           | Rapid, thin                               |
| **Treatment Principle** | Clear Lung heat, nourish fluids           |

Note: [Lung Heat Wei is characterized by upper limb weakness, fever, and dry cough. The tongue is red with a thin yellow coating, and the pulse is rapid and thin. Treatment focuses on clearing Lung heat and nourishing fluids.]

## Pattern Differentiation: Spleen & Stomach Deficiency Wei

| Characteristic      | Description                                          |
| :------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| **Key Symptoms**   | Weakness in all limbs, fatigue, poor appetite       |
| **Tongue**          | Pale, swollen with teeth marks                       |
| **Pulse**           | Weak, thin                                           |
| **Treatment Principle** | Strengthen Spleen & Stomach, tonify Qi & Blood     |

Note: [Spleen & Stomach Deficiency Wei is characterized by weakness in all four limbs, fatigue, and poor appetite. The tongue is pale and swollen with teeth marks, and the pulse is weak and thin. Treatment focuses on strengthening the Spleen and Stomach and tonifying Qi and Blood.]

## Pattern Differentiation: Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei

| Characteristic      | Description                                                                        |
| :------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| **Treatment Principle** | Nourish Liver and Kidney Yin, strengthen bones and tendons                        |

Note: [Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei is characterized by lower limb weakness, lower back pain, and dizziness. The tongue is red and dry, and the pulse is thin and weak, especially at the rear position. Treatment focuses on nourishing Liver and Kidney Yin and strengthening bones and tendons.]

## Pattern Differentiation: Damp-Heat Wei

| Characteristic      | Description                                                |
| :------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
