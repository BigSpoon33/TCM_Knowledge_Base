## 4. Etiology & Pathomechanisms

**Question:**
What causes Etiology & Pathomechanisms and how does it develop?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
**Etiology & Pathomechanisms**
**Primary Causes (Etiology)**
*   **Deficiency of Lung Qi:** The Lungs govern Qi and respiration, influencing muscle strength and vitality. Chronic respiratory conditions, prolonged illness, or congenital weakness can weaken Lung Qi, leading to insufficient nourishment of the muscles.
*   **Spleen and Stomach Dysfunction:** The Spleen and Stomach are responsible for transforming and transporting Qi and Blood, the foundation for muscle nourishment. Poor diet, overwork, chronic digestive issues, or emotional stress can impair Spleen and Stomach function, resulting in insufficient Qi and Blood production.
*   **Liver Blood Deficiency:** The Liver governs the sinews and stores Blood. Insufficient Liver Blood can lead to malnourishment of the tendons and ligaments, contributing to weakness and atrophy. Blood loss, chronic illness, or emotional stagnation can deplete Liver Blood.

## 8. Acupuncture Points

**Question:**
Which acupuncture points would you use for Acupuncture Points?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
**General Principles for Point Selection:**
*   **Local Points:** Points located directly on or near the affected muscles and limbs are used to stimulate local Qi and Blood circulation.
*   **Distal Points:** Points located on the limbs, distant from the affected area, but along the same meridian are used to regulate meridian Qi flow.
*   **Meridian Selection:** Points are selected based on the affected meridians. For example, weakness in the legs may involve the Spleen, Stomach, Liver, or Kidney meridians.
*   **Pattern Differentiation:** Points are chosen based on the specific TCM pattern diagnosis (e.g., Spleen Qi deficiency, Liver Blood deficiency).
