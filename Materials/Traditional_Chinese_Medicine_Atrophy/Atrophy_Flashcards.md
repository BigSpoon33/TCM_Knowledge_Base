# Atrophy Flashcards

## Atrophy
What is Wei Zheng in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)?
?
A syndrome characterized by progressive muscle weakness, muscle atrophy, and potentially paralysis. "Wei" signifies a wilting or weakening.

**Flashcard 2:**

---
## Atrophy
Name the primary etiological factors contributing to Wei Zheng according to TCM.
?
Deficiency of Qi and Blood, Liver and Kidney Deficiency, Damp-Heat Obstruction, Phlegm Obstruction, Trauma.

**Flashcard 3:**

---
## Atrophy
What is the role of the Spleen and Stomach in the context of Wei Zheng?
?
They are responsible for transforming food into Qi and Blood, which nourish the muscles and tendons. Dysfunction can lead to deficiency and subsequent muscle atrophy.

**Flashcard 4:**

---
## Atrophy
How does Liver and Kidney deficiency contribute to Wei Zheng?
?
Liver governs tendons and sinews; Kidneys store essence (Jing) for bone and marrow nourishment. Deficiencies weaken muscles and lead to atrophy.

## Atrophy
What are the key symptoms of Damp-Heat Wei?
?
Heaviness and weakness in the limbs, muscle pain, swelling, limited range of motion, possible skin lesions.

**Flashcard 6:**

## Atrophy
What tongue and pulse presentation is characteristic of Spleen & Stomach Deficiency Wei?
?
Tongue: Pale, swollen with teeth marks. Pulse: Weak, thin.

## Atrophy
What is the treatment principle for Liver & Kidney Deficiency Wei?
?
Nourish Liver and Kidney Yin, strengthen bones and tendons.

## Atrophy
Give an example of an herbal formula used for Spleen Qi deficiency in the treatment of Wei Zheng.
?
Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang (Tonify the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction).

## Atrophy
How does acupuncture help in treating Wei Zheng?
?
Acupuncture points are selected to tonify Qi and Blood, clear Damp-Heat or Phlegm, nourish the Liver and Kidneys, or activate Blood circulation, based on the specific pattern.

**Flashcard 10:**

## Atrophy
What lifestyle modifications are important in the long-term management of Wei Zheng?
?
Maintain a healthy diet, engage in regular exercise (within limitations), manage stress, and seek ongoing support from a qualified TCM practitioner.
