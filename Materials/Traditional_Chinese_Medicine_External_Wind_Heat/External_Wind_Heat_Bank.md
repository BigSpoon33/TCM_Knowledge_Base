# External Wind Heat Question Bank

### Question 1

**Which of the following is the PRIMARY cause of External Wind Heat in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)?**

**Correct Answer:** Exposure to Wind and Heat

**Explanation:** The primary cause of External Wind Heat is exposure to environmental Wind carrying Heat. This is more common in warmer seasons but can occur anytime.

**Options:**
- Exposure to Dampness
- Exposure to Wind and Heat
- Internal Deficiency of Yin
- Emotional Stress leading to Liver Qi Stagnation

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following is a key characteristic differentiating Wind Heat from Wind Cold?**

**Correct Answer:** Sore throat

**Explanation:** Sore throat is a prominent feature of Wind Heat, while it's usually absent or mild in Wind Cold. Also, in Wind Heat, fever is higher than aversion to cold.

**Options:**
- Clear, watery nasal discharge
- Pronounced aversion to cold, more than fever
- Sore throat
- Aversion to heat

### Question 3

**According to TCM, what is the INITIAL part of the body affected by External Wind Heat?**

**Correct Answer:** The Lung and Wei Qi

**Explanation:** Wind Heat typically invades through the nose, mouth, and skin, initially affecting the Lung and Wei Qi. The Lung governs Qi and controls the skin and nasal passages, making it a primary target. Wei Qi is responsible for defending the body's exterior.

**Options:**
- The Liver and Gallbladder
- The Spleen and Stomach
- The Lung and Wei Qi
- The Kidney and Bladder

### Question 4

**Which of the following is a commonly used herb in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) to treat External Wind Heat?**

**Correct Answer:** *Jin Yin Hua* (Honeysuckle Flower)

**Explanation:** *Jin Yin Hua* (Honeysuckle Flower) is one of the commonly used herbs to treat External Wind Heat. Other herbs include *Lian Qiao* (Forsythia Fruit), *Bo He* (Mint), *Ju Hua* (Chrysanthemum Flower), *Sang Ye* (Mulberry Leaf), and *Lu Gen* (Reed Rhizome).

**Options:**
- *Gui Zhi* (Cinnamon Twig)
- *Fu Ling* (Poria)
- *Jin Yin Hua* (Honeysuckle Flower)
- *Dang Gui* (Angelica Root)

### Question 5

**Which of the following dietary recommendations is MOST appropriate for someone with External Wind Heat?**

**Correct Answer:** Focus on light, easily digestible foods like congee and steamed vegetables.

**Explanation:** It's best to avoid spicy, greasy, and processed foods, which can generate more Heat. Focus on light, easily digestible foods like congee, soups, and steamed vegetables. Consume cooling foods like pears, apples, and watermelon (in moderation).

**Options:**
- Consume plenty of warming spices like ginger and cinnamon.
- Eat primarily raw, cold foods to directly cool the body.
- Focus on light, easily digestible foods like congee and steamed vegetables.
- Eat lots of red meat to strengthen the Qi.
