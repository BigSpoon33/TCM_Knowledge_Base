# External Wind Heat Flashcards

## External Wind Heat
What are the two primary pathogenic factors involved in External Wind Heat?
?
Wind and Heat. Wind carries the Heat into the body.

**Flashcard 2**

---
## External Wind Heat
What are some common clinical manifestations (signs and symptoms) of External Wind Heat?
?
High fever, slight aversion to cold, sore throat, headache, yellow nasal discharge, red tongue with thin yellow coating, superficial and rapid pulse.

**Flashcard 3**

---
## External Wind Heat
How does Wei Qi relate to susceptibility to External Wind Heat?
?
Weakened Wei Qi (Defensive Qi) makes one more susceptible to External Wind Heat invasion.

**Flashcard 4**

---
## External Wind Heat
How does External Wind Heat typically enter the body?
?
Through the nose, mouth, and skin, initially affecting the Lung and Wei Qi.

---
## External Wind Heat
What are the key diagnostic differences between External Wind Heat and External Wind Cold?
?
In Wind Heat, fever is higher than aversion to cold; sore throat is prominent; nasal discharge and sputum are yellow and thick.  In Wind Cold, aversion to cold is more pronounced; sore throat is mild or absent; nasal discharge and sputum are clear and watery.

## External Wind Heat
What is the primary treatment principle for External Wind Heat?
?
Dispel Wind, clear Heat, and release the exterior.

## External Wind Heat
Name three commonly used herbs in treating External Wind Heat.
?
*Jin Yin Hua* (Honeysuckle Flower), *Lian Qiao* (Forsythia Fruit), *Bo He* (Mint).

## External Wind Heat
Give an example of a classic herbal formula used for External Wind Heat.
?
*Yin Qiao San* (Honeysuckle and Forsythia Powder).

## External Wind Heat
Name three commonly used acupuncture points for treating External Wind Heat and their rationale.
?
LI-4 (Hegu) and LI-11 (Quchi) clear heat and release the exterior. LU-7 (Lieque) disperses wind and benefits the lungs. DU-14 (Dazhui) clears heat from the upper body. GB-20 (Fengchi) expels wind. Tai Yang reduces fever and headache.

## External Wind Heat
What are some dietary recommendations for someone with External Wind Heat?
?
Avoid spicy, greasy, and processed foods. Focus on light, easily digestible foods like congee, soups, and steamed vegetables. Consume cooling foods like pears, apples, and watermelon (in moderation).
