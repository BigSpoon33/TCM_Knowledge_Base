# taiyang headache Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), where is a Taiyang headache primarily located?**

**Correct Answer:** Posterior head and neck region

**Explanation:** A Taiyang headache in TCM is specifically located in the posterior head and neck region, corresponding to the Taiyang meridian pathway.

**Options:**
- Forehead and temples
- Around the eyes and sinuses
- Posterior head and neck region
- Top of the head

### Question 2

**Which of the following is a common sensation associated with Taiyang headaches?**

**Correct Answer:** Throbbing, sharp, dull, stiff, or tight pain

**Explanation:** Patients may describe the pain of a Taiyang headache as throbbing, sharp, dull, stiff, or tight, depending on the underlying disharmony.

**Options:**
- Burning pain
- Throbbing, sharp, dull, stiff, or tight pain
- Itching and tingling
- Electric shock-like sensations

### Question 3

**According to TCM, what is a Taiyang headache often an indication of?**

**Correct Answer:** Invasion of external pathogenic factors (like Wind or Cold)

**Explanation:** A Taiyang headache often indicates that external pathogenic factors, such as Wind or Cold, are attacking the Taiyang channel, the outermost defensive layer of the body.

**Options:**
- Kidney Deficiency
- Invasion of external pathogenic factors (like Wind or Cold)
- Spleen Qi Deficiency
- Heart Fire
