# Brain Fog Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), brain fog is understood as a disruption of what fundamental aspect?**

**Correct Answer:** Disharmonies impacting the *Shen* (Spirit/Mind) and Zang-Fu organ functions.

**Explanation:** TCM views brain fog symptoms as manifestations of underlying energetic imbalances affecting the *Shen* and the proper functioning of internal organs.

**Options:**
- The balance of neurotransmitters in the brain.
- A deficiency in essential vitamins and minerals.
- Disharmonies impacting the *Shen* (Spirit/Mind) and Zang-Fu organ functions.
- Inflammation of the brain tissue.

### Question 2

**Which of the following is NOT a common TCM pattern associated with brain fog?**

**Correct Answer:** Lung Yin Deficiency

**Explanation:** The TCM patterns commonly associated with brain fog are Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness Accumulation, Kidney Jing Deficiency, Heart Blood Deficiency, Liver Qi Stagnation, and Phlegm Misting the Mind. Lung Yin Deficiency is not mentioned as a primary cause.

**Options:**
- Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness Accumulation
- Kidney Jing Deficiency
- Heart Blood Deficiency
- Lung Yin Deficiency

### Question 3

**A patient with brain fog complains of fatigue, bloating, loose stools, and a sticky taste in the mouth. Which TCM pattern is MOST likely?**

**Correct Answer:** Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness Accumulation

**Explanation:** The symptoms of fatigue, bloating, loose stools, and a sticky taste in the mouth are classic indicators of Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness in TCM.

**Options:**
- Liver Qi Stagnation
- Heart Blood Deficiency
- Kidney Jing Deficiency
- Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness Accumulation

### Question 4

**Which of the Four Pillars of TCM Diagnosis involves evaluating the quality, strength, and rhythm of the pulse?**

**Correct Answer:** Palpation (切)

**Explanation:** Palpation, specifically pulse diagnosis, is one of the four pillars used to determine organ imbalances by assessing the pulse's quality, strength, and rhythm.

**Options:**
- Observation (望)
- Auscultation and Olfaction (闻)
- Inquiry (问)
- Palpation (切)

### Question 5

**Which tongue presentation is MOST suggestive of Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness?**

**Correct Answer:** Pale, swollen with a greasy coating

**Explanation:** A pale, swollen tongue with a greasy coating is a key diagnostic sign of Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness in TCM tongue diagnosis.

**Options:**
- Red and dry
- Pale, swollen with a greasy coating
- Thin and red
- Normal color, no coating
