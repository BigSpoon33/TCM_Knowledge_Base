# Brain Fog Flashcards

## Brain Fog
In Western medicine, is "brain fog" a recognized disease or syndrome?
?
No, it's a descriptive term for a collection of symptoms, not a specific diagnosis.

**Flashcard 2**

---
## Brain Fog
List three common underlying TCM patterns associated with symptoms of brain fog.
?
Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness, Kidney Jing Deficiency, Heart Blood Deficiency. (Accept: Liver Qi Stagnation, Phlegm Misting the Mind as additional answers).

**Flashcard 3**

---
## Brain Fog
What is the role of the *Shen* in TCM's understanding of brain fog?
?
Brain fog indicates disruption of the clear function of *Shen* (Spirit/Mind) due to underlying imbalances.

**Flashcard 4**

---
## Brain Fog
Name the Four Pillars of TCM Diagnosis.
?
Observation (望), Auscultation and Olfaction (闻), Inquiry (问), and Palpation (切).

**Flashcard 5**

---
## Brain Fog
Describe the typical mental and emotional symptoms associated with Liver Qi Stagnation that might contribute to brain fog.
?
Irritability, frustration, mood swings, and difficulty concentrating due to stress.

**Flashcard 6**

---
## Brain Fog
What type of physical symptoms are most commonly seen with Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness that are related to brain fog?
?
Fatigue, poor appetite, bloating, loose stools, edema, and a sticky taste in the mouth.

**Flashcard 7**

---
## Brain Fog
What kind of tongue would you expect to see in a patient suffering from Phlegm Misting the Mind?
?
Swollen tongue with a greasy coating.

**Flashcard 8**

---
## Brain Fog
What pulse quality is typical in Kidney Jing Deficiency?
?
Thready, weak, especially at the rear positions.

**Flashcard 9**

---
## Brain Fog
Besides asking general health questions, what specific type of questions is important when diagnosing brain fog in TCM?
?
Questions about the timing of the symptoms, factors that make them better or worse (diet, stress, activity), digestive issues, and sleep patterns.

**Flashcard 10**

---
## Brain Fog
How does *Qi* and Blood Deficiency contribute to brain fog in TCM?
?
It leads to inadequate nourishment of the brain.
