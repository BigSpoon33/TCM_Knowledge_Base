# Blood Stasis Pattern (Xue Yu 瘀血)

Foundational TCM Pattern

Qi-Blood-Body Fluids Category

---

## Learning Objectives

- Identify cardinal symptoms
- Differentiate from Qi Stagnation
- Select appropriate treatments
- Explain pathomechanisms

---

## What is Blood Stasis?

- Blood flow slows, pools, or obstructs
- Excess condition (often from Deficiency)
- Causes pain, masses, serious disease

---

## Four Cardinal Symptoms

- Fixed, stabbing pain
- Purple tongue/spots
- Dark complexion
- Choppy/hesitant pulse

---

## Blood Stasis vs Qi Stagnation

- Pain: Fixed/Stabbing vs Moving/Distending
- Tongue: Purple vs Normal/Red sides
- Pulse: Choppy vs Wiry

---

## How Blood Stasis Forms (Qi Stagnation)

- Stress → Qi Stagnation
- Qi fails to move Blood
- Blood pools and stagnates
- Pain and purple signs appear

---

## Treatment Principles

- Invigorate Blood
- Transform Stasis
- Move Qi
- Stop Pain

---

## Key Formulas

- Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang (upper body)
- Shao Fu Zhu Yu Tang (lower body)
- Tao Hong Si Wu Tang (with Blood Def)

---

## Clinical Case

- Chest pain, stabbing, fixed
- Purple lips, choppy pulse
- Diagnosis: Heart Blood Stasis
- Treatment: Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang

---

## Key Takeaways

- Fixed stabbing pain = Blood Stasis
- Purple tongue confirms diagnosis
- Treat with blood-invigorating formulas
- Caution in pregnancy
