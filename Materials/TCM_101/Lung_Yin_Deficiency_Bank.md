# Lung Yin Deficiency Question Bank

### Question 1

**Which of the following is a fundamental characteristic of Lung Yin Deficiency in TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Dryness of the respiratory tract

**Explanation:** Dryness, deficiency heat, and impaired Lung function are the fundamental characteristics of Lung Yin Deficiency. The lack of fluids leads to a dry respiratory tract.

**Options:**
- Excessive phlegm production
- Dryness of the respiratory tract
- Excessive cooling of the body
- Strengthened Lung function

---
### Question 2

**In TCM, which organ is considered the 'root of Yin' and often interconnected with Lung Yin Deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidneys

**Explanation:** The Kidneys are considered the root of Yin in the body, and chronic Lung Yin Deficiency can deplete the Kidney Yin. The Kidneys are said to 'nourish' the Lungs with fluids.

**Options:**
- Liver
- Heart
- Kidneys
- Spleen

---
### Question 3

**Which of the following is a common general symptom associated with Lung Yin Deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Dry mouth and throat

**Explanation:** Dry mouth and throat, especially experiencing thirst in the evening, is a common general symptom of Lung Yin Deficiency due to the lack of fluids.

**Options:**
- Profuse sweating during the day
- Feeling cold, especially in the extremities
- Dry mouth and throat
- Excessive salivation

---
### Question 4

**What tongue presentation is most indicative of Lung Yin Deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Red, especially at the tip, with a scanty or peeled coating

**Explanation:** A red tongue, especially at the tip, with a scanty, absent, or peeled coating is indicative of Lung Yin Deficiency. The red color reflects heat, and the lack of coating indicates a deficiency of fluids.

**Options:**
- Pale with a thick, white coating
- Red, especially at the tip, with a scanty or peeled coating
- Swollen with teeth marks
- Purple with a greasy yellow coating

---
### Question 5

**Which of the following pulse qualities is most likely to be observed in a patient with Lung Yin Deficiency?**

**Correct Answer:** Thin and rapid

**Explanation:** The pulse quality associated with Lung Yin Deficiency is typically thin (Xi), indicating a deficiency of blood and fluids, and rapid (Shu), suggesting heat.

**Options:**
- Forceful and bounding
- Thin and rapid
- Slippery and wiry
- Slow and deep
