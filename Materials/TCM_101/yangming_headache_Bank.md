# yangming headache Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), Yangming Headache is primarily localized in which region of the head?**

**Correct Answer:** The forehead region

**Explanation:** Yangming Headache is characterized by pain primarily localized in the forehead region, stemming from imbalances within the Yangming channels.

**Options:**
- The occipital region (back of the head)
- The temporal region (sides of the head)
- The parietal region (top of the head)
- The forehead region

### Question 2

**Which of the following channels is MOST associated with Yangming Headache?**

**Correct Answer:** The Stomach and Large Intestine channels

**Explanation:** Yangming Headache stems from imbalances within the Yangming channels, specifically the Stomach (足陽明胃經) and Large Intestine (手陽明大腸經) channels.

**Options:**
- The Liver channel
- The Kidney channel
- The Stomach and Large Intestine channels
- The Heart channel

### Question 3

**Which of the following symptoms is commonly associated with Yangming Headache?**

**Correct Answer:** Constipation

**Explanation:** Yangming Headache is often associated with signs of heat, such as thirst, aversion to heat, constipation, and a flushed face.

**Options:**
- Aversion to cold
- Clear nasal discharge
- Constipation
- Pale face

### Question 4

**According to the provided text, what pulse quality is MOST associated with Yangming headache?**

**Correct Answer:** Rapid, forceful, slippery or wiry

**Explanation:** The pulse diagnosis for Yangming headache typically reveals a rapid, forceful, slippery (hua – 滑) or wiry (xian – 弦) pulse, especially strong in the right Guan (關) position.

**Options:**
- Slow and weak
- Rapid, forceful, slippery or wiry
- Deep and thready
- Floating and empty

---
### Question 5

**Which of the following is an aggravating factor for Yangming headache?**

**Correct Answer:** Exposure to heat

**Explanation:** Aggravating factors for Yangming headache include heat (exposure to sun, hot weather, hot drinks/foods), hunger or skipping meals, and specific foods (spicy, greasy, or rich foods).

**Options:**
- Cold compress to the forehead
- Eating (if related to hunger)
- Exposure to heat
- Rest
