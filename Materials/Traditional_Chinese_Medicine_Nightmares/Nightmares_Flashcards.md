# Nightmares Flashcards

## Nightmares
What is the TCM perspective on nightmares ( *È Mèng, Mèng Yǎn,* or *Jīng Mèng*)?
?
They are disturbances of the *Shen* (spirit/mind) indicating an underlying imbalance in the body, rather than just random bad dreams.

**Flashcard 2**

---
## Nightmares
Where does the *Shen* reside, and what functions does it govern?
?
The *Shen* resides in the Heart and governs mental clarity, emotional balance, and restful sleep.

**Flashcard 3**

---
## Nightmares
What are some common clinical manifestations of nightmares from a TCM perspective?
?
Recurrent frightening dreams, sudden awakenings, palpitations, sweating, difficulty falling back asleep, daytime fatigue, irritability, poor concentration, and anxiety.

---
## Nightmares
What is the focus of TCM treatment for nightmares?
?
Identifying and addressing the underlying pattern causing the *Shen* disturbance, rather than just the dream content.

## Nightmares

Fatigue, irritability, poor concentration, and anxiety.

## Nightmares
Name three TCM patterns that can be associated with nightmares.
?
Heart-Blood Deficiency, Liver-Fire Rising, Spleen Deficiency with Phlegm, and Kidney-Yin Deficiency.
