# Nightmares Question Bank

### Question 1

**According to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), nightmares are primarily viewed as a disturbance of what?**

**Correct Answer:** The Shen (神)

**Explanation:** In TCM, nightmares are seen as significant disturbances of the Shen (神), often translated as spirit or mind, which resides in the Heart and is responsible for mental clarity, emotional balance, and restful sleep.

**Options:**
- The Lung meridian
- The digestive system
- The Shen (神)
- The flow of Qi

### Question 3

**Which of the following is NOT a common sign or symptom of nightmares from a TCM perspective?**

**Correct Answer:** Excessive hunger during the day

**Explanation:** Common signs and symptoms from a TCM perspective include recurrent frightening dreams, sudden awakening with palpitations, difficulty falling back asleep, daytime fatigue, irritability, and anxiety.

**Options:**
- Recurrent, frightening dreams
- Sudden awakening with palpitations
- Difficulty falling back asleep
- Excessive hunger during the day

### Question 4

**Which TCM pattern is associated with vivid, disturbing nightmares featuring anger or aggression?**

**Correct Answer:** Liver-Fire Rising

**Explanation:** Liver-Fire Rising is associated with vivid, disturbing nightmares with anger or aggression, as well as irritability and a short temper.

**Options:**
- Heart-Blood Deficiency
- Liver-Fire Rising
- Spleen Deficiency with Phlegm
- Kidney-Yin Deficiency

### Question 5

**A patient experiencing bizarre, frightening nightmares with nonsensical plots, along with a swollen tongue with teeth marks, may be diagnosed with which TCM pattern?**

**Correct Answer:** Spleen Deficiency with Phlegm

**Explanation:** Spleen Deficiency with Phlegm is associated with bizarre, frightening nightmares, excessive dreaming, and a swollen, pale tongue with teeth marks.

**Options:**
- Heart-Blood Deficiency
- Liver-Fire Rising
- Spleen Deficiency with Phlegm
