# Eczema Flashcards

## Eczema
According to TCM, what is the fundamental perspective on eczema compared to Western medicine?
?
TCM views eczema as a manifestation of internal imbalances and disharmony within the body, focusing on the root cause rather than solely treating symptoms.

## Eczema
Which three Zang-Fu (organ) systems are most commonly implicated in eczema according to TCM?
?
Lungs, Spleen, and Liver.

## Eczema
How does Lung Qi deficiency contribute to eczema, according to TCM?
?
Lung Qi deficiency can lead to dry skin and increased susceptibility to external pathogens.

## Eczema
Describe the key features of the "Wind-Heat" pattern of eczema in TCM.
?
Erythematous papules and vesicles with rapid onset, intense itching, burning sensation, possible fever, red tongue with thin yellow coating, and floating, rapid pulse.

## Eczema
What type of skin lesions are characteristic of the "Damp-Heat" pattern in acute eczema?
?
Erythematous, edematous, weeping vesicles, and crusts.

## Eczema
What are the main characteristics of the "Blood Deficiency with Wind" pattern in chronic eczema?
?
Dry, thickened, lichenified, scaly skin, persistent itching (worse at night), pale complexion, dizziness, blurred vision, pale dry tongue, and thin, weak pulse.

## Eczema
What dietary recommendations might a TCM practitioner give to someone with a "Damp-Heat" pattern of eczema?
?
Avoid greasy, fried foods, dairy, and sugary drinks.

Give an example of an herb commonly used in TCM formulas to treat the "Wind-Heat" pattern and explain its action.
?

## Eczema
Name three acupuncture points that are commonly used in TCM to treat eczema and explain their functions.
?
SP 6 (Sanyinjiao): Strengthens the Spleen and resolves Dampness. LI 11 (Quchi): Clears Heat and dispels Wind. LV 3 (Taichong): Moves Liver Qi and calms Wind.
