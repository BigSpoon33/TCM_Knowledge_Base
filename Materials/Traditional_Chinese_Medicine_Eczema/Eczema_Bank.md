# Eczema Question Bank

### Question 1

**According to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), eczema is viewed as:**

**Correct Answer:** A manifestation of internal imbalances and disharmony within the body.

**Explanation:** TCM views eczema holistically, seeing it as a symptom of underlying imbalances within the body's organ systems and Qi/Blood flow, rather than a localized skin disease.

**Options:**
- A single disease with a specific cause.
- Primarily a skin condition requiring topical treatment.
- A manifestation of internal imbalances and disharmony within the body.
- An allergic reaction requiring antihistamines.

### Question 2

**Which of the following organ systems, according to TCM, is most directly responsible for the skin's ability to regulate moisture and defend against external pathogens?**

**Correct Answer:** The Lungs

**Explanation:** In TCM, the Lungs control the skin and its ability to regulate moisture and defend against external factors. Lung Qi deficiency can lead to dry skin and increased susceptibility to external pathogens.

**Options:**
- The Liver
- The Spleen
- The Lungs
- The Kidneys

### Question 3

**In the acute stage of eczema, the 'Wind-Heat' pattern is characterized by which of the following?**

**Correct Answer:** Erythematous papules and vesicles with rapid onset and intense itching.

**Explanation:** The Wind-Heat pattern in acute eczema is characterized by the rapid onset of itchy, red papules and vesicles. The 'Wind' aspect implies sudden onset and migratory nature, while 'Heat' indicates redness, burning, and potential thirst.

**Options:**
- Dry, thickened skin with fissures.
- Erythematous, edematous, weeping vesicles.
- Erythematous papules and vesicles with rapid onset and intense itching.
- Scaling and lichenification with persistent oozing.

### Question 4

**Which of the following herbs is commonly used in TCM to treat the 'Blood Deficiency with Wind' pattern of eczema?**

**Correct Answer:** Dang Gui (Angelica Root)

**Explanation:** Dang Gui (Angelica Root) is an herb that nourishes Blood and moistens dryness, making it suitable for treating Blood Deficiency with Wind, which is a common chronic eczema pattern.

**Options:**
- Huang Lian (Coptis Rhizome)
- Jin Yin Hua (Honeysuckle Flower)
- Dang Gui (Angelica Root)
- Fu Ling (Poria)

### Question 5

**For a patient diagnosed with the 'Damp-Heat' pattern of eczema, which dietary recommendation is MOST appropriate according to TCM principles?**

**Correct Answer:** Avoid greasy, fried foods, dairy, and sugary drinks.

**Explanation:** In TCM, Damp-Heat is aggravated by foods that promote Dampness and Heat in the body. Therefore, greasy, fried foods, dairy, and sugary drinks should be avoided.

**Options:**
- Consume plenty of dark leafy greens to nourish the Blood.
- Avoid greasy, fried foods, dairy, and sugary drinks.
- Increase intake of warming foods like ginger and cinnamon.
- Eat more raw vegetables to clear Heat.
