# Vertigo Question Bank

### Question 1

**From a TCM perspective, vertigo is primarily caused by:**

**Correct Answer:** Imbalance within the body's internal organ systems.

**Explanation:** TCM views vertigo as a systemic issue stemming from an imbalance within the body's organ systems, disrupting the flow of clear yang to the head.

**Options:**
- Inner ear dysfunction.
- Disturbance of blood circulation to the brain.
- Imbalance within the body's internal organ systems.
- Inflammation of the vestibular nerve.

---
### Question 2

**Spinning vertigo, characterized by an intense sensation of rotation, most strongly suggests which TCM pattern?**

**Correct Answer:** Liver Wind Rising.

**Explanation:** Intense spinning vertigo is a key indicator of Liver Wind Rising or Phlegm-Fire disturbing the head according to TCM.

**Options:**
- Qi and Blood Deficiency.
- Kidney Essence Deficiency.
- Liver Wind Rising.
- Phlegm Dampness.

---
### Question 3

**High-pitched tinnitus is most indicative of which TCM pattern in the context of vertigo?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidney Deficiency.

**Explanation:** High-pitched tinnitus, especially if constant or intermittent, is a common symptom associated with Kidney Deficiency in TCM.

**Options:**
- Liver Fire.
- Liver Wind Rising.
- Kidney Deficiency.
- Phlegm-Fire.

### Question 4

**A throbbing, temporal headache accompanying vertigo suggests which TCM pattern?**

**Correct Answer:** Liver Wind.

**Explanation:** A throbbing, often temporal headache is a typical symptom of Liver Wind in TCM when associated with vertigo.

**Options:**
- Qi and Blood Deficiency.
- Kidney Deficiency.
- Phlegm Dampness.
- Liver Wind.

---
### Question 5

**Gradual onset vertigo is more likely to be associated with which of the following TCM patterns?**

**Correct Answer:** Qi and Blood Deficiency

**Explanation:** Gradual onset vertigo typically suggests deficiency patterns like Qi and Blood Deficiency or Kidney Deficiency, as opposed to the sudden onset seen with excess patterns like Liver Wind Rising or Phlegm-Fire.

**Options:**
- Liver Wind Rising
- Phlegm-Fire
- Qi and Blood Deficiency
- All of the above
