# Impotence Question Bank

### Question 1

**In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), what is impotence, also known as Erectile Dysfunction (ED), called?**

**Correct Answer:** Yang Wei

**Explanation:** In TCM, impotence is called *Yang Wei*, which translates to 'flaccid yang'.

**Options:**
- Yin Yang Huo
- Yang Wei
- Huangdi Neijing
- Ming Men

---
### Question 2

**Which of the following is considered the MOST common cause of impotence in TCM?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidney Yang Deficiency

**Explanation:** Kidney Yang Deficiency is considered the most common cause of impotence in TCM.  It provides the vital energy and warmth necessary for sexual function.

**Options:**
- Liver Qi Stagnation
- Heart Blood Deficiency
- Kidney Yang Deficiency
- Damp-Heat in the Lower Jiao

---
### Question 3

**According to TCM, what emotional factor can lead to Liver Qi Stagnation, potentially causing impotence?**

**Correct Answer:** Stress and Frustration

**Explanation:** Emotional factors such as stress, anger, and frustration, can impede the smooth flow of Liver Qi, leading to Liver Qi Stagnation.  This stagnation can prevent the proper nourishment of the genitals, leading to erectile dysfunction.

**Options:**
- Joy
- Fear
- Stress and Frustration
- Grief

---
### Question 4

**Which of the following treatment principles is used in TCM to address the emotional component of impotence, relieving stress and frustration?**

**Correct Answer:** Soothing Liver Qi and Promoting its Smooth Flow

**Explanation:** Soothing Liver Qi and Promoting its Smooth Flow addresses the emotional component of impotence, relieving stress and frustration. Herbal formulas often contain herbs like Chai Hu (Bupleurum).

**Options:**
- Tonifying Kidney Yang and Jing
- Soothing Liver Qi and Promoting its Smooth Flow
- Nourishing Heart Blood and Calming the Shen
- Clearing Damp-Heat

### Question 5

**A patient presents with erectile dysfunction, cold limbs, lower back pain, and frequent urination (especially at night). According to TCM, which pattern is MOST likely?**

**Correct Answer:** Kidney Yang Deficiency

**Explanation:** The symptoms of cold limbs, lower back pain, and frequent urination (especially at night) are characteristic of Kidney Yang Deficiency in TCM.

**Options:**
- Liver Qi Stagnation
- Kidney Yang Deficiency
