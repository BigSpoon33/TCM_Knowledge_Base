##  Contraindications & Cautions

**Treatment Contraindications:**
- Use caution with strongly draining or purging therapies.
- Avoid overstimulation in extremely deficient patients.
- Address the root deficiency before treating branch symptoms excessively.
- Monitor patient response closely, adjusting treatment as needed.
- Avoid foods that further weaken the deficient system (e.g., cold foods for Yang deficiency, spicy foods for Yin deficiency).

**Cautions:**
- Start with low doses of herbs and gradually increase as tolerated.
- Use caution with acupuncture in extremely deficient patients, using gentle stimulation.

**When to Avoid:**
- Avoid using strong purging herbs or acupuncture techniques in cases of severe deficiency.

## ‚úÖ Clinical Pearls & Experience

### Clinical Tips
- Always assess the patient's overall condition and identify the specific deficiency involved (Qi, Yang, Blood, Yin).
- Consider the patient's age, lifestyle, and medical history when making a diagnosis and developing a treatment plan.
- Start with gentle treatments and gradually increase the intensity as tolerated.

### Common Mistakes
- Treating the symptoms without addressing the underlying deficiency.
- Using overly strong treatments that further deplete the body's resources.

### Treatment Modifications
- If the patient has concurrent Dampness, add herbs and acupuncture points to drain Dampness.
- If the patient has concurrent Qi stagnation, add herbs and acupuncture points to regulate Qi.

### Success Indicators
- Improved energy levels and reduced fatigue.
- Increased appetite and improved digestion.
- Warmer limbs (if Yang deficiency).
- Reduced night sweats (if Yin deficiency).
