## HEAT-COLD

### HEAT

Red complexion, fever, thirst with preference for cold drinks, constipation, deep-yellow and scanty urine.
T: Red tongue with yellow and dry coating.
P: Rapid pulse.

---

## EXCESS-HEAT (FULL-HEAT)

**Symptoms:**
- Whole face red
- Desire to drink cold water
- Red all over inside eyelid
- Bitter taste
- Feeling heat all day
- High fever
- Very restless and agitated
- Constipation, abdominal pain
- Profuse bleeding
- Dream-disturbed, very restless sleep
- Red-hot-painful skin eruption

**Tongue:** Red with yellow coating

**Pulse:** Excess-rapid-surging

**Treatment Principle:** Clear heat

**Etiology:**
- Emotional problems
- Diet (too much red meat, spices, alcohol)
- An external pathogenic factor that has penetrated into the Interior and transformed into Heat

---

## DEFICIENCY-HEAT (EMPTY-HEAT)

**Symptoms:**
- Malar flush
- Desire to drink warm water, or cold water in small sips
- Thin red line inside eyelid
- No bitter taste
- Feeling heat in the afternoon or evening
- Low-grade fever
- Vague anxiety, fidgeting
- Dry stools, no abdominal pain
- Slight bleeding
- Waking up frequently during the night or early morning
- Scarlet-red, not raised, painless skin eruptions

**Tongue:** Red and peeled or thin

**Pulse:** Superficial-deficiency-rapid

**Treatment Principle:** Nourish Yin, clear deficient-heat

**Etiology:**
- Overwork (in the sense of working long hours)
- Irregular eating
- Excessive sexual activity
- Persistent, heavy blood loss (such as in menorrhagia)

---

### COLD

Pallor, aversion to cold, absence of thirst or drinking a little hot drinks, loose stools, clear urine increased in volume.
**Tongue:** Pale tongue with white and moist coating.
**Pulse:** Slow pulse.

---

## EXCESS-COLD (FULL-COLD)

**Symptoms:**
- Bright-white
- Sharp pain, worse on pressure
- Better after bowel movement

**Tongue:** Thick-white coating

**Pulse:** Excess-tight-deep

**Etiology:**
- Cold foods (salads, fruit, iced drinks)
- External Cold that penetrates into the Interior

---

## DEFICIENCY-COLD (EMPTY-COLD)

**Symptoms:**
- Sallow-white
- Dull pain, better on pressure
- Worse after bowel movement

**Tongue:** Thin-white coating

**Pulse:** Weak-slow-deep

**Etiology:**
- Excessive physical work
- Diet, i.e. inadequate consumption of hot foods
- Excessive sexual activity causing KD Yang deficiency
- Internal Cold injuring Yang

## TRUE/FALSE HEAT AND COLD

---

## TRUE COLD + FALSE HEAT

**LOOKING:**
- Red cheeks, but red color is like powder, rest of face white; irritability but also listlessness, desire to lie with body curled-up.
**Tongue:** Pale and wet tongue

**HEARING:**
- Breathing quiet, low voice

**ASKING:**
- Thirst but no desire to drink, or desire to drink warm fluids, body feels hot but he or she likes to be covered; sore throat but without redness or swelling; pale urine

**FEELING:**
**Pulse:** Rapid, superficial and big but empty

---

## TRUE HEAT + FALSE COLD

**LOOKING:**
- Dark face, bright eyes with spirit, red-dry lips, irritability.
**Tongue:** Strong body tongue, red, dry

**HEARING:**
- Breathing noisy, loud voice

**ASKING:**
- Thirst with desire to drink cold fluids; scanty-dark urine, constipation, burning sensation in anus

**FEELING:**
**Pulse:** Deep, excess, cold limbs but chest is hot

## EXCESS-DEFICIENCY

### EXCESS (FULL)

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Sturdiness, agitation, sonorous voice, coarse breathing, distension and fullness in the chest and abdomen, pain aggravated by pressure, constipation or tenesmus, dysuria.

**Tongue:** Thick and sticky tongue coating (①)

**Pulse:** Pulse of excess type (®)

**Additional Notes:** The presence of a pathogenic factor while the Upright Qi is relatively intact.

### DEFICIENCY (EMPTY)

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Emaciation, listlessness, lassitude, feeble breathing, dislike of speaking, pallor, palpitations, shortness of breath, insomnia, poor memory, spontaneous and night sweating, nocturnal emission, nocturnal enuresis, pain alleviated by pressure.

**Tongue:** Dry tongue with no coating or little coating (①)

**Pulse:** Pulse of deficiency type (®)

**Additional Notes:** Emptiness (Deficiency) of the Upright Qi and the absence of a pathogenic factor.

## TYPES OF DEFICIENCY

### QI DEFICIENCY

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Pale face, weak voice, slight sweating, slight shortness of breath, tiredness, loose stools, a poor appetite.

**Pulse:** Empty (®)

### YANG DEFICIENCY

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Chilliness, a bright pale face, cold limbs, absence of thirst, a desire for hot drinks, loose stools, frequent pale urination.

**Tongue:** Pale and wet (①)

**Pulse:** Weak (®)

### BLOOD DEFICIENCY

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Dull-pale face, pale lips, blurred vision, dry hair, tiredness, poor memory, numbness or tingling, insomnia, scanty periods.

**Tongue:** Pale-Thin (①)

**Pulse:** Fine or Choppy (®)

### YIN DEFICIENCY

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Feeling of heat in the afternoon or evening, a dry throat at night, night sweating, a thin body.

**Tongue:** Red-Peeled and dry (①)

**Pulse:** Floating-Empty (®)

- Coldness of the body
- Cold hands and feet
- Feeble breathing
- Listlessness

**Tongue (CAM):** Pale and Moist

**Pulse (CAM):** Thready and Fading

**Symptoms (FCM):**
- Chilliness
- Cold limbs
- Weak breathing
- Profuse sweating with an oily sweat
- Absence of thirst
- Frequent profuse urination or incontinence
- Loose stools or incontinence

**Tongue (FCM):** Pale-Wet-Swollen-Short

**Pulse (FCM):** Minute-Deep

- Feverishness of the body

**Pulse (CAM):** Thready, Rapid and Weak
