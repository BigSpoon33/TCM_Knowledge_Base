##  Contraindications & Cautions

**Treatment Contraindications:**
- Using strong draining methods in patients with significant Qi or Blood Deficiency.
- Overly cold or bitter herbs in patients with weak digestive function, as this can further damage the Spleen.

**Cautions:**
- Monitor the patient's digestive function closely during treatment.
- Adjust the herbal formula based on the patient's individual response and any emerging symptoms.

**When to Avoid:**
- Avoid using strong purgative herbs if the patient is pregnant or has a history of Spleen Qi Deficiency.

## âœ… Clinical Pearls & Experience

### Clinical Tips
- Always inquire about the patient's dietary habits and emotional state.
- Consider the patient's constitutional type when formulating a treatment plan.
- Palpate the right hypochondriac region to assess the degree of distention and tenderness.

### Common Mistakes
- Neglecting the underlying Spleen Qi Deficiency when treating Gallbladder Damp.
- Using excessively cold or bitter herbs, which can further damage the Spleen.

### Treatment Modifications
- **If patient presents with significant emotional stress:** Add herbs to soothe the Liver Qi, such as Chai Hu (Bupleurum).
- **If patient presents with more heat signs:** Add herbs to clear heat, such as Huang Qin (Scutellaria baicalensis).

### Success Indicators
- Improvement in digestive symptoms such as nausea and aversion to fatty foods.
- Resolution of right hypochondriac pain or distention.
