## üîç Clinical Manifestations

### Cardinal Symptoms (CAM)
**Essential Symptoms:**
- Epigastric stuffiness and fullness
- Fatigue
- Thick, sticky tongue coating
- Rolling and string-taut pulse
- Poor appetite

### Complete Symptom Picture

**Chief Symptoms:**
- Epigastric stuffiness and fullness, often described as a feeling of pressure or distension after eating
- Fatigue and lethargy, especially after meals
- Poor appetite and aversion to greasy foods

**Accompanying Symptoms:**
- Nausea, sometimes with vomiting
- Feeling of heaviness in the limbs or head
- Thirst with a lack of desire to drink much, or only in small sips
- Sallow complexion, possibly with slight jaundice
- Hypochondriac pain or discomfort
- Bitter taste in the mouth, especially in the mornings
- Loose stools or diarrhea
- Abdominal distension, especially after eating
- Poor concentration and mental fog
- Irritability and mood swings

**Tongue:** `Thick-sticky-yellow coating, may be swollen with teeth marks`

**Pulse:** `Rolling (slippery) and string-taut (wiry)`

**vs. [[Liver Fire Attacking the Stomach]]:**
- Key difference: Liver Fire Attacking the Stomach presents with more pronounced epigastric pain, acid reflux, and a rapid pulse, indicating heat. Spleen Damp + Liver Qi Deficiency is characterized by dampness and a rolling pulse, with less intense pain.

**vs. [[Spleen Yang Deficiency]]:**
- Key difference: Spleen Yang Deficiency involves cold symptoms such as cold limbs, aversion to cold, and a pale, swollen tongue. Spleen Damp + Liver Qi Deficiency presents with dampness symptoms and a wiry pulse indicative of Liver Qi stagnation, without pronounced cold signs.

##  Contraindications & Cautions

**Treatment Contraindications:**
- Avoid damp-producing foods (dairy, sweets, greasy foods)
- Avoid overeating
- Avoid alcohol
- Caution with draining or strongly moving herbs in excessively weak patients

**Cautions:**
- Avoid prolonged use of draining herbs, as they can further weaken the Spleen.
- Monitor the patient's digestion closely and adjust the formula accordingly.

**When to Avoid:**
- When there is a clear presence of Yin Deficiency Heat

## ‚úÖ Clinical Pearls & Experience

### Clinical Tips
- Ask detailed questions about the patient's diet and emotional state.
- Observe the tongue coating carefully, as it is a key indicator of dampness.
- Palpate the abdomen for areas of tenderness or distension.

### Common Mistakes
- Focusing solely on tonifying the Spleen without addressing the Liver Qi stagnation.
- Using overly drying herbs without considering the patient's overall constitution.

### Treatment Modifications
- **If Damp-Heat is present:** Add herbs to clear heat and drain dampness, such as Long Dan Cao (Gentian Root).
- **If Liver Blood Deficiency is present:** Add herbs to nourish Liver Blood, such as Dang Gui (Angelica Sinensis).

### Success Indicators
- Improved digestion and appetite
- Increased energy levels
- Reduced emotional irritability
- Thinning of the tongue coating
