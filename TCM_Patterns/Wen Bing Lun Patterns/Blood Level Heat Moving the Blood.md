### Cardinal Symptoms (CAM)
**Essential Symptoms:**
- High fever
- Mental restlessness
- Manic behaviour

- Epistaxis

### Complete Symptom Picture

**Chief Symptoms:**
- High fever, often spiking and difficult to reduce
- Bleeding from various orifices (nose, mouth, stools, urine)
- Mental restlessness and agitation

**Accompanying Symptoms:**
- Thirst and desire for cold drinks
- Dry mouth and throat
- Insomnia
- Red eyes
- Constipation with dry stools
- Agitation and irritability

**vs. [[Ying Stage Heat]]:**
- Key difference: Ying Stage Heat has less bleeding and more focus on Yin deficiency symptoms, while Blood Level Heat has profuse bleeding and severe heat signs.

**vs. [[Liver Fire Blazing Upwards]]:**
- Key difference: Liver Fire Blazing Upwards is primarily characterized by symptoms related to Liver Qi stagnation and ascending heat, with less emphasis on systemic bleeding as seen in Blood Level Heat.

## ‚úÖ Clinical Pearls & Experience

### Clinical Tips
- Look for a dark-red tongue without coating as a key indicator of Blood level heat.
- Palpate the pulse carefully to assess its strength and quality.
- Monitor vital signs closely to detect any signs of shock or deterioration.

### Common Mistakes
- Misdiagnosing as a deficiency pattern and using warming herbs, which can exacerbate the heat.
- Neglecting to address the underlying cause of the heat, leading to recurrence of the pattern.

### Treatment Modifications
- **If Yin deficiency is prominent:** Increase the dosage of Yin-nourishing herbs.
- **If bleeding is severe:** Use herbs with strong hemostatic properties.

### Success Indicators
- Reduction in fever and bleeding
- Improvement in mental clarity
- Moistening of the tongue
