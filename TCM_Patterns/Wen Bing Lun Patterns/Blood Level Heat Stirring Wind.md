##  Contraindications & Cautions

**Treatment Contraindications:**
- Avoid using warming or tonifying herbs during the acute phase of high fever
- Do not use strong purging herbs that could further damage Yin
- Avoid needling strongly stimulating points in frail patients

**Cautions:**
- Closely monitor the patient's vital signs and mental status
- Adjust the treatment based on the patient's individual presentation and response
- Ensure adequate hydration and nutrition

**When to Avoid:**
- Avoid treating this pattern solely with acupuncture if the patient is severely ill. Combine with herbal medicine.

## ‚úÖ Clinical Pearls & Experience

### Clinical Tips
- Take a detailed history to identify any underlying imbalances or predisposing factors.
- Observe the patient's mental state and neurological signs closely.
- Choose the appropriate herbal formula and acupuncture points based on the patient's specific presentation.

### Common Mistakes
- Delaying treatment while waiting for a definitive diagnosis. This pattern requires immediate intervention.
- Focusing solely on Heat clearing without addressing the underlying Yin deficiency.
- Using overly aggressive Wind-extinguishing herbs that could further damage the body's resources.

### Treatment Modifications
- **If the patient has a weak constitution:** Use gentler Heat-clearing herbs and focus on nourishing Yin.
- **If the patient is elderly:** Be cautious with strong Wind-extinguishing herbs, as they can be too stimulating.
- **If the patient is pregnant:** Use herbs that are safe during pregnancy and avoid strong Wind-extinguishing herbs.

### Success Indicators
- Reduction in fever and convulsions
- Improvement in mental state and level of consciousness
- Moistening of the tongue and restoration of a coating
- Slowing down of the pulse
