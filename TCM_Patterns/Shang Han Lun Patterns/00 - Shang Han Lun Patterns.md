This page discusses the differentiation and treatment principles based on the Six Stages (Shang Han Lun) theory in Traditional Chinese Medicine.

---

## 7. SHANG HAN LUN DIFFERENTIATION

### DIAGNOSIS CAM

---

## Tai Yang Wind Attack

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Fever
- Aversion to wind
- Stiff neck
- Aches in the body
- Sweating

**Tongue:** Thin, white coating

**Pulse:** Superficial, Moderate

---

## Tai Yang Cold Attack

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Fever
- Aversion to cold
- Stiff neck
- Headache
- Severe pain in the body
- No sweating
- Shortness of breath

**Tongue:** Thin, white coating

**Pulse:** Superficial, Tight

---

## Yang Ming Channel Pattern

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- High fever
- Profuse sweating
- Aversion to heat
- Thirst with a desire to drink cold water
- Red face
- Restlessness

**Tongue:** Red tongue + yellow coating

**Pulse:** Surging, Rapid

---

## Yang Ming Organ Pattern

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- High fever which is worse in the afternoon
- Profuse sweating
- Constipation
- Thirst with a desire to drink cold water
- Fullness and pain in the abdomen which becomes worse on pressure
- Restlessness and irritability
- In severe cases, delirium

**Tongue:** Red tongue + a dry, yellow or black coating

**Pulse:** Deep, Excess

---

## Shao Yang

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Alternation of chills and fever
- Fullness of the costal and hypochondriac regions
- Lack of appetite
- Irritability
- Dry throat
- Nausea
- Bitter taste
- Blurred vision

**Tongue:** White-slippery tongue coating on the side only

**Pulse:** String-taut

---

## Tai Yin

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Abdominal fullness
- Vomiting
- No appetite
- Diarrhea
- Absence of thirst

**Tongue:** Pale tongue

**Pulse:** Deep, Slow

---

## Shao Yin Cold Pattern

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Chills
- Aversion to cold
- Listlessness
- Lethargy
- Cold limbs
- Diarrhea
- No thirst or desire to drink warm fluids
- Abundant-pale urine

**Tongue:** Pale tongue

**Pulse:** Deep, Thready

---

## Shao Yin Heat Pattern

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Fever
- Irritability
- Insomnia
- Dry mouth and throat
- Scanty-dark urine

**Tongue:** Red tongue without coating

**Pulse:** Thready, Rapid

---

## Jue Yin

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Thirst
- Feeling of energy rising to the chest
- Pain and feeling of heat in the chest
- Feeling of hunger with no desire to eat
- Cold limbs
- Diarrhea and vomiting

**Tongue:** Red thorns or Slippery white coat

**Pulse:** Deep, String-taut

---

### TREATMENT CAM

---

## Tai Yang Wind Attack - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Relieve Exterior, Expel Wind, Harmonize Ying and Wei

**Acupuncture:** DU, SI, UB

**Herbal Formula:** Gui Zhi Tang

**Wen Bing:** Wei

---

## Tai Yang Cold Attack - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Relieve Exterior, Diaphoresis

**Acupuncture:** (Not specified)

**Herbal Formula:** Ma Huang Tang

**Wen Bing:** Wei

---

## Yang Ming Channel Pattern - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Clear Heat, Promote Body Fluids

**Acupuncture:** LI, ST, DU

**Herbal Formula:** Bai Hu Tang

**Wen Bing:** Qi

---

## Yang Ming Organ Pattern - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Clear Heat, Purge Excess, Relieve Constipation

**Acupuncture:** Front-Mu and Lower He-sea of LI and ST, SP

**Herbal Formula:** Cheng Qi Tang (Da/Xiao/Tiao)

**Wen Bing:** Qi

---

## Shao Yang - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Harmonize the Shao Yang

**Acupuncture:** SJ, GB, PC, LV

**Herbal Formula:** Xiao Chai Hu Tang

**Wen Bing:** (Not specified)

---

## Tai Yin - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Warm Middle Jiao, Disperse Cold

**Acupuncture:** Back-Shu, Front-Mu and He-sea points of SP and ST, Ren (Needle + Moxa)

**Herbal Formula:** Li Zhong Wan

**Wen Bing:** (Not specified)

---

## Shao Yin Cold Pattern - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Restore Yang

**Acupuncture:** Ren, KD, SP (Moxa + Needle)

**Herbal Formula:** Si Ni Tang, Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan, Zhen Wu Tang

**Wen Bing:** (Not specified)

---

## Shao Yin Heat Pattern - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Nourish Yin, Clear Deficiency Heat

**Acupuncture:** HT, KD

**Herbal Formula:** Huang Lian E Jiao Tang

**Wen Bing:** Ying

---

## Jue Yin - Treatment

**Treatment Principle:** Regulate Heat and Cold simultaneously, Warm and Harmonize ST

**Acupuncture:** LV, Ren, GB, SP

**Herbal Formula:** Wu Mei Wan

**Wen Bing:** (Not specified)

---

**Additional Notes:**

# SHANG HAN LUN COMPREHENSIVE CHART

---

## TAI YANG - WIND prevalence

**Symptoms:**
- Slight aversion to cold
- Aversion to wind
- Slight fever
- Slight sweating
- Headache
- Stiff neck
- Sneezing

**Pulse:** Floating, Slow

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** UB12, LU7, LI4, GB20, SJ5, ST36
- **Formula:** Gui Zhi Tang

---

## TAI YANG - COLD prevalence

**Symptoms:**
- Aversion to cold
- Slight fever
- Absence of sweating
- Headache
- Stiff neck
- Sneezing
- Runny nose with white discharge
- Breathlessness

**Pulse:** Floating-Tight

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** UB12, LU7, LI4, SJ5, GB20, Moxa
- **Formula:** Ma Huang Tang

---

## TAI YANG - WATER accumulation

**Symptoms:**
- Aversion to cold
- Fever
- Retention of urine
- Slight thirst
- Vomiting of fluids soon after drinking

**Pulse:** Floating-Rapid

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** RN9, RN3, ST28, LU7, UB22, UB39, UB64
- **Formula:** Wu Ling San

---

## TAI YANG - BLOOD accumulation

**Symptoms:**
- Hypogastric distension
- Fullness and urgency
- Blood in urine
- Mental restlessness

**Tongue:** Reddish-Purple tongue without coating

**Pulse:** Deep-Fine-Rapid or Deep-Choppy

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** RN3, KD14, ST28, UB39, UB22, SP10, LV3, SP6
- **Formula:** Tao He Cheng Qi Tang

---

## YANG MING - CHANNEL PATTERN

**Symptoms:**
- High fever
- Profuse sweating
- Intense thirst
- Red face
- Feeling of heat
- Irritability
- Delirium

**Tongue:** Red tongue with yellow coating

**Pulse:** Overflowing-Rapid or Big-Rapid pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** LI11, DU14, PC3, ST44, ST43
- **Formula:** Bai Hu Tang

---

## YANG MING - ORGAN PATTERN

**Symptoms:**
- High fever that is worse in the afternoon
- Profuse sweating (on limbs)
- Abdominal fullness and pain
- Constipation
- Dry stools
- Thirst
- Dark urine

**Tongue:** Red tongue with thick dry yellow coating

**Pulse:** Deep-Full-Slippery-Rapid pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** LI11, DU14, PC3, ST44, ST43, ST25, SP15, ST37, SP6
- **Formula:** Cheng Qi Tang (Da / Xiao / Tiao Wei)

---

## SHAO YANG

**Symptoms:**
- Alternation of shivers (or cold feeling) and fever (or a feeling of heat)
- Bitter taste
- Dry throat
- Blurred vision
- Hypochondrial fullness and distension
- Lack of desire to eat or drink
- Irritability
- Nausea
- Vomiting

**Tongue:** Unilateral thin white coating

**Pulse:** Wiry-Fine pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** SJ5, SJ6, GB41, DU13
- **Formula:** Xiao Chai Hu Tang

---

## TAI YIN

**Symptoms:**
- Abdominal fullness
- A feeling of cold
- Vomiting
- Lack of appetite
- Diarrhoea
- Absence of thirst
- Tiredness

**Tongue:** Pale tongue with sticky white coating

**Pulse:** Deep-Weak-Slow pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** RN12, UB20, ST36, ST25, SP6, Moxa
- **Formula:** Li Zhong Tang

---

## SHAO YIN - COLD TRANSFORMATION

**Symptoms:**
- Chills
- A feeling of cold
- Lying with body curled
- Listlessness
- Desire to sleep
- Cold limbs
- Diarrhoea
- Absence of thirst
- Frequent pale urination

**Tongue:** Pale and wet tongue with white coating

**Pulse:** Deep-Weak-Slow pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** UB23, RN4, RN6, RN8, KD7, KD3, Moxa
- **Formula:** Si Ni Tang

---

## SHAO YIN - HEAT TRANSFORMATION

**Symptoms:**
- A feeling of heat
- Irritability
- Insomnia
- Dry mouth and throat at night
- Dark urine
- Night sweating

**Tongue:** Red tongue without coating

**Pulse:** Fine-Rapid pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** RN4, RN6, KD3, KD6, SP6
- **Formula:** Huang Lian E Jiao Tang

---

## JUE YIN

**Symptoms:**
- Persistent thirst
- A feeling of energy rising to the chest
- Pain and heat sensation in heart region
- Hunger but no desire to eat
- Cold limbs
- Diarrhoea
- Vomiting
- Vomiting of roundworms

**Pulse:** Wiry pulse

**Treatment Principles/Notes:**
- **Acupuncture:** LV3, LI4, SP4, PC6
- **Formula:** Wu Mei Wan
