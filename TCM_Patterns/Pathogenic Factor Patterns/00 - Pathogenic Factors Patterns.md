## DIAGNOSIS 105

## 4. PATHOGENIC FACTORS DIFFERENTIATION

### CHARACTERISTICS

1.  6 Pathogenic factors: Wind, Cold, Dampness, Heat, Dryness and Fire.
2.  They always correspond to a EXCESS pattern according to the 8 principles.
3.  Pathogenic factors are more important as patterns of disharmony than as causes of disease.
4.  The diagnosis of pathogenic factors is made not on the basis of the patient's history, but on the basis of the pattern of symptoms and signs presented.

## SIX PATHOGENS AND SYMPTOMS (pg.29)

---

### WIND

**6 CLIMATES:** WIND
**+/-:** +
**5 ELEMENT:** LV
**SEASON:** SPRING

**Symptoms (Skin):**
- Aversion to cold
- Fever
- Sore throat
- Sneezing
- Runny nose
- Occipital stiffness
- Floating pulse

**Symptoms (Channel):**
- Stiffness
- Rigidity
- Sudden muscle contraction

**Symptoms (Joint):**
- Joint pain that moves from place to place, esp. in the upper part of the body (Wind-Bi)

---

### FIRE

**6 CLIMATES:** FIRE
**+/-:** +

**SEASON:** SUMMER

- Mental confusion
- Thirst

### DAMPNESS

**6 CLIMATES:** DAMPNESS
**+/-:** -
**5 ELEMENT:** SP
