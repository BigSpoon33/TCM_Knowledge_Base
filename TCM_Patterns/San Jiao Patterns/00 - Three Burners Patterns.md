# 9. THREE BURNERS DIFFERENTIATION

---

## DAMP-HEAT IN UPPER JIAO

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Severe aversion to cold
- mild fever or absence of fever
- a heavy sensation in the head as if it were tightly wrapped by a cloth
- heaviness of the limbs and trunk
- a stifling sensation in the chest
- absence of thirst
- dull facial expression
- epigastric fullness and distension
- poor appetite
- borborygmus
- loose stools

**Tongue:** T: white, sticky tongue coating
**Pulse:** P: soft, slowing-down pulse

---

## DAMP-HEAT IN MIDDLE JIAO

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Indistinct at the first touch of the skin, but becomes pronounced after being felt for a rather long time
- or fever which recurs after reduced by sweating
- or fever which is more pronounced in the afternoon
- heaviness of the limbs and trunk
- distension and fullness in the chest and epigastrium
- nausea
- vomiting
- anorexia
- thirst with desire to drink only a little
- scanty and deep-yellow urine
- loose but hesitant stools
- and in severe cases, dull facial expression with few words said or mental cloudiness

**Tongue:** T: sticky white coating + yellow tinge
**Pulse:** P: soft, rapid pulse

---

## DAMP-HEAT IN LOWER JIAO

**Symptoms (CAM):**
- Retention of urine
- thirst with desire to drink only a little
- constipation
- hardness and fullness in the lower abdomen

**Tongue:** T: sticky yellow or white coating
**Pulse:** P: soft, rapid pulse
