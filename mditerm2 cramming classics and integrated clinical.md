# XiaoKe

Upper Wasting (Xiao-Ke) Syndrome is characterized by 
?
Lung heat drying body fluids, resulting in symptoms such as fidgeting, polydipsia, a dry red tongue (with or without cracks) with a thin, yellow coat, and a forceful, rapid, pulse (especially at the cun position). 

---

?

---

?
Stomach fire damaging the fluids, leading to such symptoms as polyphagia, constant hunger with good appetite, a red tongue with a yellow coat, and a slippery, forceful, rapid pulse. 

---

Patients with Middle Wasting (Xiao-Ke) Syndrome should combine 
?
Equilibrium with Rehmannia & Gypsum Combination (Yu Nu Jian). 

---

by such symptoms

## Master Shi Jin-Mo’s Seven Coupled Herb Sets for Diabetes (_Xiao Ke_)

### 1. Atractylodes (_Cang Zhu_) & Figwort Root (_Xuan Shen_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Atractylodes is bitter, warm, and drying, specializing in invigorating the Spleen and drying dampness. Figwort Root is salty, cold, moist, and specializes in nourishing Yin and draining Fire. The pair is balanced: the moisture of Figwort Root controls the dryness of Atractylodes, and the warm dryness of Atractylodes controls the cloying nature of Figwort Root.
    
- **Main Indication:** To regulate the Middle Palace (Spleen/Stomach), stop leakage and turbidity, and **lower blood glucose**.
    
- **Dosage:** Atractylodes 10–15g; Figwort Root 15–30g.
    
---

### 2. Pueraria Root (_Ge Gen_) & Salvia Root (_Dan Shen_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Pueraria Root generates fluids, stops thirst, dilates blood vessels, improves circulation, and lowers blood sugar. Salvia Root moves Blood stasis, generates new blood, cools blood, sedates, and lowers blood sugar. Together, they promote blood circulation and eliminate stasis.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes) with a focus on **lowering blood glucose**, especially indicated for patients showing signs of **Blood stasis** (dark tongue, petechiae, or sublingual vein stagnation).
    
- **Dosage:** Pueraria Root 10–15g; Salvia Root 10–15g.
    
---

### 3. Mung Bean Hull (_Lu Dou Yi_) & Job's Tears Seed (_Yi Yi Ren_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Mung Bean Hull is light and cold, clearing heat toxicity from the organs and skin. Job's Tears Seed drains dampness, clears Lung heat, and strengthens the Spleen to transform dampness. Together, they benefit the Spleen and Stomach, promote transformation, and clear deficiency/toxic heat.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes), especially for symptoms corresponding to **Upper Wasting** (excessive thirst/heat).
    
- **Dosage:** Mung Bean Hull 6–10g; Job's Tears Seed 10–15g.
    
---

### 4. Astragalus Root (_Huang Qi_) & Yam Rhizome (_Shan Yao_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Astragalus is sweet and warm, tonifying Qi and raising Yang, with a focus on **Spleen Yang**; Yam is sweet and neutral, nourishing Yin and stabilizing essence, with a focus on **Spleen Yin**. Combined, this Yin-Yang pair strengthens the Spleen and Stomach, promotes transportation, and stabilizes the Spleen essence.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes) for eliminating **glucosuria (sugar in the urine)**.
    
- **Dosage:** Astragalus Root 10–30g; Yam Rhizome 10–30g.
    
---

### 5. Anemarrhena Rhizome (_Zhi Mu_) & Gypsum (_Shi Gao_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Anemarrhena is sweet, bitter, and cold, moisturizing and draining Fire from the Lung, Stomach, and lower burner (empty Fire). Gypsum is sweet, acrid, and very cold, specifically clearing **excess heat from the Qi level** in the Lung and Stomach. Together, they significantly clear and drain heat from the Lung and Stomach.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes) characterized by **Upper Wasting** symptoms of dry mouth, thirst, and excessive drinking. (Also for external febrile diseases with internal Qi-level heat).
    
- **Dosage:** Anemarrhena Rhizome 6–10g; Gypsum 15–30g (crushed, decocted first).
    
---

### 6. Phellodendron Bark (_Huang Bai_), Anemarrhena Rhizome (_Zhi Mu_), & Cinnamon Bark (_Rou Gui_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Anemarrhena and Phellodendron (the "Two-Eighty" method) nourish Yin, drain Fire, and clear damp-heat from the urinary bladder. The addition of warm, acrid Cinnamon Bark acts as a guide, leading the cold herbs to the lower body, enhancing their ability to nourish Yin, drain Fire, and **clear damp-heat from the Lower Jiao**.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes) corresponding to **Lower Wasting**, especially effective for severe symptoms like frequent urination, cloudy urine, or urine that looks like grease/tallow.
    
- **Dosage:** Phellodendron Bark 6–10g; Anemarrhena Rhizome 6–10g; Cinnamon Bark 2–4g.
    
---

### 7. Figwort Root (_Xuan Shen_) & Ophiopogon Root (_Mai Dong_)
?
- **Combined Function:** Figwort Root (black, Kidney) nourishes Yin and drains Fire; Ophiopogon Root (white, Lung/Stomach) clears the Heart, moistens the Lung, and generates fluids. Representing "Metal generates Water," the Lung and Kidney relationship is utilized to achieve **"Metal-Water mutual generation,"** strongly nourishing Yin, generating fluids, and stopping thirst.
    
- **Main Indication:** _Xiao Ke_ (Diabetes) presenting with symptoms of **Yin deficiency** such as dry mouth, excessive thirst, and a red tongue with scant coating.
    
- **Dosage:** Figwort Root 10–30g; Ophiopogon Root 10–15g.

# Wind Stroke

What is the general Western medical term for the conditions corresponding to a TCM Wind-stroke? 
?
Cerebra-vascular accident (CVA), also known as apoplexy or "stroke".

---

What four Western medical conditions correspond to the TCM term "Wind-stroke"? 
?

1. Cerebral hemorrhage
    
2. Cerebral thrombosis
    
3. Cerebral embolism
    
4. Spasm of a cerebral vessel
    

---

What three common lifestyle factors lead to Kidney-yin deficiency, a root cause of Wind-stroke? 
?

1. Overwork (long hours, stress)
    
2. Emotional strain
    
3. Excessive sexual activity
    

---

How does Kidney-yin deficiency lead to internal Wind? 
?
Kidney-yin deficiency leads to Liver-yin deficiency, which causes Liver-yang to rise. Rising Liver-yang gives rise to Liver-wind.

---

What are the tongue signs of Liver-wind? 
?
A moving, deviated, or stiff tongue.

---

What dietary habits weaken the Spleen and lead to Phlegm, a factor in Wind-stroke? 
?
Irregular eating or excessive amounts of fats, dairy foods, greasy/fried foods, and sugar.

---

What are the symptoms associated with Phlegm in Wind-stroke? 
?
Numbness in the limbs, mental cloudiness, slurred speech, or aphasia.

---

What are the tongue signs of Phlegm? 
?
A swollen tongue with a sticky coating.

---

How can Kidney-essence deficiency lead to Blood stasis? 
?
It leads to Marrow deficiency, which fails to nourish Blood, eventually leading to Blood stasis.

---

What are the symptoms and tongue signs of Blood stasis? 
?
Symptoms: Stiffness and pain in the limbs. Tongue: A purple tongue body.

---

What are the four main pathogenic factors involved in the pathogenesis of Wind-stroke? 
?
Wind, Phlegm, Fire, and Stasis.

---

What is the tongue appearance for the pathogenic factor: Wind? 
?
Stiff, moving, deviated, or quivering.

---

What is the tongue appearance for the pathogenic factor: Phlegm? 
?
Swollen, with a sticky coating.

---

What is the tongue appearance for the pathogenic factor: Fire? 
?
A red body.

---

What is the tongue appearance for the pathogenic factor: Stasis? 
?
A reddish-purple body.

---

What is the most important differentiation in Wind-stroke? 
?
Whether it attacks the internal organs AND channels (severe type) or only the channels (mild type).

---

What are the distinguishing signs of an attack on the internal organs by Wind (severe type)? 
?
Loss of consciousness, coma, and aphasia.

---

What characterizes a Wind-stroke attack on the channels alone (mild type)? 
?
Unilateral paralysis, numbness, and slurred speech, with NO loss of consciousness.

---

The severe type (attack of internal organs) is further divided into what two patterns? 
?
The Tense (or Closed) type and the Flaccid (or Open) type.

---

What are the key signs of the Tense (Closed) type of Wind-stroke? 
?
Sudden collapse, loss of consciousness, clenched teeth, closed fists, lock-jaw, red face, and coarse breathing.

---

What is the tongue and pulse for the Tense (Closed) type? 
?
Tongue: Red body, Stiff, Deviated, with a sticky-yellow coating. Pulse: Wiry-full-rapid-slippery.

---

What are the key signs of the Flaccid (Open) type of Wind-stroke? 
?
Sudden collapse, loss of consciousness, open hands and mouth, pale face, oily sweat, incontinence of stools/urine, and cold limbs.

---

What is the tongue and pulse for the Flaccid (Open) type? 
?
Tongue: Pale, swollen. Pulse: Minute-hidden-scattered.

---

What are the symptoms of an attack on the _main_ channels (mild type)? 
?
Facial paralysis, hemiplegia, numbness of limbs, limitation of movement, and slurred speech.

---

What are the symptoms of an attack on the _connecting_ channels alone? 
?
Unilateral numbness of face and limbs, and slurred speech.

---

What acupuncture points are used to relieve spasm in an attack on the channels? 
?
P-6 (Neiguan) and SP-6 (Sanyinjiao).

---

DU-26 (Renzhong) and LI-4 (Hegu).

LI-11 (Quchi), ST-36 (Zusanli), LIV-3 (Taichong), and KI-3 (Taixi).

---

What is the treatment principle for the Tense (Closed) type? 
?
Induce resuscitation, relax spasm, clear heat, subdue wind, resolve phlegm, and open the orifices.

---
