# **Dang Gui Bu Xue Tang** (Tangkuei Decoction to Tonify the Blood, Angelica Blood-Tonifying Decoction, Tokishigyakukato)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classic example of "qi is the commander of blood, blood is the mother of qi." The 5:1 ratio (Huang Qi 30g : Dang Gui 6g) demonstrates that qi deficiency is the root cause of blood deficiency. When qi is strong, it can generate and move blood properly. Simple but profound formula based on fundamental TCM physiology.

# **Dang Gui Shao Yao San** (Tangkuei and Peony Powder, Angelica and Peony San, Tokishakuyakusan)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classic women's formula that addresses the common pattern of blood deficiency with liver-spleen disharmony. The combination nourishes blood while addressing the underlying digestive weakness that often accompanies women's health issues. Particularly important for pregnancy-related symptoms where gentle, safe treatment is essential.

# **Gui Pi Tang** (Restore the Spleen Decoction, Ginseng and Longan Combination, Kibitang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Gui Pi" means "restore the spleen" - emphasizing that the spleen is the source of qi and blood production. Classic formula for the heart-spleen relationship where spleen weakness leads to blood deficiency, which then disturbs the heart spirit. Essential pattern includes both physical weakness and mental-emotional symptoms.

# **Shao Yao Gan Cao Tang** (Peony and Licorice Decoction, Bai Shao Gan Cao Tang, Shaoyao Gancao Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Shao Yao Gan Cao" represents the simplest yet most effective combination for liver-spleen disharmony with muscle tension. Classic for "jin ji" (muscle urgency/spasms) where liver yin deficiency leads to internal wind and muscle contractions. The sweet-sour combination nourishes yin while harmonizing qi.

# **Si Wu Tang** (Four Substances Decoction, Tang-kuei Four Combination)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Mother of all blood formulas" - the foundation for treating blood deficiency in TCM. The four herbs work in perfect harmony: two nourish (Dang Gui, Shu Di), one preserves (Bai Shao), one moves (Chuan Xiong). Represents the classic approach to building blood without causing stagnation.

# **Tao Hong Si Wu Tang** (Four Substances Decoction with Peach Kernel and Safflower, Tangkuei and Peach Kernel Combination)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Perfect formula for blood deficiency complicated by blood stasis. The Si Wu Tang base nourishes blood while Tao Ren and Hong Hua gently move blood and resolve stasis. Represents the balanced approach to treating blood patterns - nourish and move simultaneously.

# **Zhi Gan Cao Tang** (Honey-Fried Licorice Decoction, Prepared Licorice Formula, Restore the Pulse Powder)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Restore the Pulse" refers to the formula's ability to regulate irregular heartbeat. Classic combination of qi tonification, yin nourishing, and blood enriching specifically for heart rhythm disorders. The honey-fried licorice provides gentle, sustained qi support.
