# **Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang** (Supplement the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction, Center-Supplementing Qi-Boosting Decoction)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Master formula for "middle qi sinking" - the concept that spleen qi becomes too weak to hold organs in place. Represents the perfect balance of tonifying without stagnation due to lifting herbs.

# **Liu Jun Zi Tang** (Six Gentlemen Decoction, Six Noble Men Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Six Gentlemen" builds on the "Four Gentlemen" (Si Jun Zi Tang) by adding phlegm-transforming herbs. Perfect formula for modern lifestyle - addresses both qi deficiency and phlegm-dampness accumulation from poor diet and sedentary habits.

# **Shen Ling Bai Zhu San** (Ginseng, Poria, and Atractylodes Powder, Shenling Baizhu San, Ten Gentlemen Powder)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Shen Ling Bai Zhu" represents comprehensive spleen qi tonification with dampness resolution. Classic formula for chronic digestive weakness with internal dampness accumulation. The ten-herb combination addresses both the root (spleen qi deficiency) and the manifestation (dampness and diarrhea). Essential for constitutional digestive weakness.

# **Sheng Mai San** (Generate the Pulse Powder, Pulse-Generating Powder, Shengmai San)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Sheng Mai" means "generate the pulse" - referring to restoring pulse strength in conditions of qi and yin collapse. Classic formula for summer heat patterns and chronic weakness with excessive fluid loss. The three-herb combination perfectly balances qi tonification, yin nourishment, and fluid preservation.

# **Si Jun Zi Tang** (Four Gentlemen Decoction, Four Noble Men Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Foundation formula for all spleen qi deficiency patterns. Represents the "four gentlemen" approach - each herb is mild, harmonious, and supportive rather than aggressive. The perfect balance for building qi without causing stagnation.

# **Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi Tang** (Aucklandia and Cardamom Six Gentlemen Decoction, Aromatic Six Gentlemen Formula, Xiangsha Liujunzi Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi" adds aromatic qi-regulating herbs to Liu Jun Zi Tang for cases where spleen qi deficiency is complicated by qi stagnation. The aromatic herbs (Mu Xiang and Sha Ren) prevent the tonifying herbs from causing stagnation while enhancing their digestive benefits. Perfect for weak digestion with bloating and distension.
