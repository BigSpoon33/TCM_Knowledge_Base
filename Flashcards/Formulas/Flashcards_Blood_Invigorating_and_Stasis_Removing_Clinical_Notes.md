# **Bu Yang Huan Wu Tang** (Tonify Yang to Restore Five-tenths Decoction, Astragalus Combination, Post-Stroke Recovery Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Restore Five-tenths" refers to the partial yang qi (five-tenths) that remains after stroke. Classic formula for post-stroke recovery when qi deficiency leads to blood stasis and paralysis. The large dose of Huang Qi is essential for lifting qi and promoting circulation.

# **Fu Yuan Huo Xue Tang** (Restore the Primordial and Invigorate Blood Decoction, Origin-Restoring Blood-Moving Decoction, Fukugenokuketsuto)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Fu Yuan" means "restore the primordial" - referring to restoring the body's original healthy state after trauma. Classic formula for injuries affecting the hypochondriac region (ribs and sides). The combination moves blood while addressing the liver qi stagnation that often accompanies trauma and pain.

# **Gui Zhi Fu Ling Wan** (Cinnamon Twig and Poria Pill, Keishibukuryogan, Cinnamon and Hoelen Formula)

Classic formula that balances warming yang qi (Gui Zhi, Fu Ling) with cooling blood-moving herbs (Mu Dan Pi, Chi Shao, Tao Ren). # **Shao Fu Zhu Yu Tang** (Drive Out Stasis in the Lower Abdomen Decoction, Lower Abdominal Blood Stasis Formula, Shaofu Zhuyu Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Shao Fu" refers to the lower abdomen, particularly the area below the navel where reproductive organs are located. "Zhu Yu" means "drive out stasis." Classic formula for blood stasis in the pelvic region with underlying cold and yang deficiency. The combination of blood-moving and warming herbs addresses both the stagnation and the underlying cold.
