# **Mai Men Dong Tang** (Ophiopogon Decoction, Liriope Root Decoction, Maimendong Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classic formula that demonstrates the principle "treat the opposite" - using Ban Xia (warm, drying) within a yin-nourishing formula. The small amount of Ban Xia descends rebellious qi without overwhelming the yin-nourishing action. Essential for chronic stomach conditions with both dryness and qi rebellion.

# **Qing Fei Tang** (Clear the Lungs Decoction, Lung-Clearing Formula, Qingfei Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Clear the Lungs" refers to clearing heat and phlegm from the lung system while preserving lung fluids. Classic approach to lung heat patterns that combines heat-clearing with phlegm transformation. Essential for respiratory conditions where heat and phlegm combine to obstruct lung function.

# **Sha Shen Mai Men Dong Tang** (Adenophora and Ophiopogon Decoction, Glehnia and Liriope Combination, Shashen Maimendong Tang)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classic formula for "late-stage febrile disease" where heat has damaged lung and stomach yin, leaving residual dryness. Essential for recovery phase of illness when pathogenic heat has cleared but yin fluids need restoration. Gentle approach that nourishes without being cloying or interfering with recovery.

# **Yu Quan Wan** (Jade Spring Pills, Jade Fountain Pills, Yuquan Wan)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Yu Quan" means "jade spring" - referring to the formula's ability to generate precious, clear fluids like a pure spring. Classic formula for Xiao Ke disease (wasting-thirst disease), which corresponds closely to modern diabetes. The five herbs work synergistically to address the three classic symptoms: excessive thirst, urination, and hunger.
