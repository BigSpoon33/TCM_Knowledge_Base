# **Liu Wei Di Huang Wan** (Six Ingredient Pill with Rehmannia, Liu Wei Di Huang Wan)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classical "mother formula" for kidney yin deficiency. The "three tonics, three drainers" structure prevents stagnation while building yin. Represents the foundation treatment for "kidney syndrome" in TCM.

# **Qi Ju Di Huang Wan** (Lycium Berry and Chrysanthemum Pill with Rehmannia, Wolfberry and Chrysanthemum Formula, Qiju Dihuang Wan)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
Classic modification of Liu Wei Di Huang Wan specifically for eye problems related to liver-kidney yin deficiency. Gou Qi Zi nourishes liver and kidney while benefiting vision, while Ju Hua clears liver heat and brightens eyes. Essential concept: "kidney stores essence which manifests in the eyes."

# **Yi Guan Jian** (Linking Decoction, One-Thread Decoction, Consistent Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Yi Guan" means "linking together" or "consistent thread" - referring to the formula's ability to simultaneously nourish yin and regulate qi. Classic approach to liver yin deficiency complicated by qi stagnation. The small amount of Chuan Lian Zi prevents the heavy yin-nourishing herbs from causing stagnation.

# **Zhi Bo (Bai) Di Huang Wan** (Anemarrhena, Phellodendron, and Rehmannia Pill, Zhibo Dihuang Wan, Knowledge and Cypress Rehmannia Pills)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Zhi Bai" refers to Zhi Mu (knowledge) and Huang Bai (cypress bark) - the two herbs added to Liu Wei Di Huang Wan to address deficiency heat. This formula treats the common progression where kidney yin deficiency leads to ministerial fire hyperactivity, causing heat symptoms. Classic for "steaming bone fever."

# **Zuo Gui Wan** (Left-Restoring Pill, Restore the Left Kidney Pill, Zuo Gui Pills)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Zuo Gui" means "restore the left" where "left" refers to kidney yin. Zhang Jing-yue's masterpiece for pure kidney yin deficiency with essence depletion. More nourishing than Liu Wei Di Huang Wan, suitable for severe yin deficiency and aging conditions.
