# **Huo Xiang Zheng Qi San** (Agastache Powder to Rectify the Qi, Patchouli Qi-Correcting Powder, Koshosan)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Zheng Qi" means "rectify the qi" - referring to correcting qi movement that has been disrupted by dampness. Classic formula for summer illnesses where external dampness combines with internal spleen weakness. The aromatic herbs awaken the spleen while releasing the exterior.

# **Ping Wei San** (Calm the Stomach Powder, Magnolia and Ginger Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Notes & Commentary**?
?
### Traditional Understanding
"Calm the Stomach" refers to settling rebellious stomach qi and transforming dampness that impairs digestion. Classic formula for spleen dampness patterns. The four herbs work together to dry dampness, move qi, and protect stomach function.
