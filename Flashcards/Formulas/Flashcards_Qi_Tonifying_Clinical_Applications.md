# **Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang** (Supplement the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction, Center-Supplementing Qi-Boosting Decoction)
## What are the details for **Clinical Applications**?
?
### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Spleen and stomach qi deficiency with qi sinking
**Key Symptoms**: Chronic fatigue, prolapse of organs (uterus, rectum, bladder), chronic diarrhea, shortness of breath, weak voice, poor appetite, dizziness upon standing
**Tongue**: Pale with thin white coating
**Pulse**: Weak, especially in the middle position, may be thready

# **Liu Jun Zi Tang** (Six Gentlemen Decoction, Six Noble Men Formula)
## What are the details for **Clinical Applications**?
?
### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Spleen qi deficiency with phlegm-dampness
**Key Symptoms**: Fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools, nausea, abdominal distension, chronic cough with white phlegm, dizziness, heavy sensation in body
**Tongue**: Pale with thick white greasy coating
**Pulse**: Weak and slippery

# **Sheng Mai San** (Generate the Pulse Powder, Pulse-Generating Powder, Shengmai San)
## What are the details for **Clinical Applications**?
?
### Primary Pattern & Manifestations
**Pattern**: Qi and yin deficiency with fluid loss and sweating
**Key Symptoms**: Profuse sweating, shortness of breath, fatigue, thirst, dry mouth, weak pulse, palpitations, restlessness
**Tongue**: Red and dry with little coating
**Pulse**: Rapid and weak, or thready and rapid
