# **SJ-15** · TIĀNLIÁO (天髎) · *Heavenly Crevice*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Oblique insertion directed according to the clinical manifestations
**Depth:** 0.5 to 1 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- perpendicular insertion, especially in thin patients, carries a substantial risk of inducing a pneumothorax

# **SJ-17** · YÌFĒNG (翳風) · *Wind Screen*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Perpendicular insertion, directed towards the opposite ear
**Depth:** 0.5 to 1 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- if the needle is directed too anteriorly or posteriorly, pain will ensue and may cause discomfort on opening and closing the mouth for some while after treatment

# **SJ-20** · JIǍOSŪN (角孫) · *Minute Angle*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Transverse insertion. May be needled anteriorly, posteriorly, inferiorly or superiorly, with direction depending on symptomatology (towards headache or other pain radiation, or to connect with other head points).
**Depth:** 0.5-1.5 cun. Inserted within the subcutaneous layers close to the bone of the skull, rather than more shallowly.
<!--SR:!2025-11-10,3,250-->

# **SJ-5** · WÀIGUĀN (外關) · *Outer Pass*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Slightly oblique insertion towards the ulnar side or oblique proximal or distal insertion towards the elbow or wrist respectively. Joined by through-needling to Nèiguān P-6.
**Depth:** 0.5 to 1.5 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- movement of the patient's arm or hand after needling this point can result in a bent needle.
<!--SR:!2025-11-10,3,250-->

# **SJ-8** · SĀNYÁNGLUÒ (三陽絡) · *Three Yang Luo*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Slightly oblique insertion towards the ulnar side or oblique proximal or distal insertion towards the elbow or wrist respectively
**Depth:** 0.5 to 1.5 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- According to the Systematic Classic of Acupuncture and Moxibustion and the Illustrated Classic of Acupuncture Points on the Bronze Man this point is contraindicated to needling.
