# **SI-1** · SHÀOZÉ (少澤) · *Lesser Marsh*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Perpendicular or oblique insertion directed proximally 0.1 to 0.2 cun, or prick to bleed.
**Depth:** 0.1 to 0.2 cun

# **SI-12** · BǏNGFĒNG (秉風) · *Grasping the Wind*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Oblique medial insertion towards the spine
**Depth:** 0.5 to 1 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- deep perpendicular insertion, especially in thin patients, carries a substantial risk of inducing a pneumo-thorax
<!--SR:!2025-11-10,3,250-->

# **SI-13** · QŪYUÁN (曲垣) · *Crooked Wall*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** i. Perpendicular insertion; ii. Oblique lateral insertion.
**Depth:** 0.3 to 0.5 cun (perpendicular); 0.5 to 1 cun (oblique lateral)

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- This point is located close to the medial border of the scapula. Too medial an insertion or deep medial-oblique needling may puncture the lungs.
<!--SR:!2025-11-10,3,250-->

# **SI-18** · QUÁNLIÁO (顴髎) · *Cheek Bone Crevice*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** i. Transverse insertion joined to points such as Dìcāng ST-4, Xiàguān ST-7, Yíngxiāng L.I.-20, Jiáchē ST-6 etc.; ii. Perpendicular insertion.
**Depth:** 0.5 to 0.7 cun

# **SI-3** · HÒUXĪ (後谿) · *Back Stream*
## What is the needling technique and cautions for this point?
?
**Method:** Perpendicular insertion, directed towards Sānjiān L.I.-3.
**Depth:** 0.5 to 2 cun

**⚠️ Cautions:**
- Needle with the hand in a loose fist position, i.e. with the metacarpal bones lying in the same plane; this is especially important when needling deeply towards the opposite side of the hand.
